# Aligned Souls — Starter Situational-Judgment Item Bank (v1 draft)

— hardened per panel review P1/P2 (2026-07-04)

**Status:** First-draft authored item bank for owner (pastoral + logotherapy) review. Extends `discernment-engine.md` (§2a SJT recipe) and `logotherapy-meaning-spine.md` (Dimension #11 + interventions). **Not yet expert-keyed or calibrated** — the "expert key" below is a *first draft* per the base doc's rule that every item needs a resourced keying panel + empirical calibration before it goes live (base §2g, D-e).

**App:** Aligned Souls — the dating app of "become first, meet second." Posture default: **faith-forward** (per base §6b), but every stem and every option is authored in **human-universal language** so a not-yet-believer answers honestly; only the *interpretation* language swaps by posture. **Domain taxonomy:** `intention`, `vulnerability`, `pace` (base §6b), extended here with meaning/attitudinal content per the spine.

---

## How to read every item (the invariant format)

Each item is one **situation** (3–5 sentences, a real relational fork), then **4–5 response options** the person **rates 1–5** (Very unlikely → Very likely — *not* "pick one"; rating each option is what resists faking and reduces social-desirability bias, base §2a). Then:

- **Expert key (first draft):** the target rating *pattern* — which options high, which low, and the diagnostic combinations. Scoring is z-standardized distance to this expert profile, **never the user's self-label** (base §2a recipe).
- **Rationale:** the construct logic, the Franklian/pastoral grounding, and the anti-faking note.

**Reading rules honored throughout:**
- **States, not traits.** Every item measures a current stance in a domain, re-sampled over time — never a fixed "type" (base §0, spine §Part II).
- **One triangulation input.** SJTs measure "knows/enacts what good looks like here"; they never stand alone — behavior, ESM, and journaling cross-check (base §2a caveat).
- **The "tell" options.** Most items include one option that, if rated high, is a **flag to re-sample** (a vacuum marker, a coercion marker, a fusion marker) — scored as a signal, never as "bad person."
- **Vacuum/meaning routing is gate-first and asymmetric — NEVER a Safety-Gate bypass (P1-2, spine §II sub-signal 6).** Several items here route a vacuum reading (e.g. A1e "the quiet feels unbearable," A3d "not being alone" as the driver) to *meaning-work*. This is safe **only** because the independent Safety Gate (base §8, recall-biased, errs toward the check-in) is **blind to the meaning/mood/vacuum distinction and evaluates EVERY input first, including every vacuum-flagged one; only inputs it CLEARS are eligible for the meaning-work routing.** "Routes to meaning-work, never to the Safety Gate" means *after* the gate has already seen and cleared it — it is **not** "vacuum language skips the gate," and must never be built as one. "What's the point / none of it matters" is a meaning marker AND a possible risk marker; the risk classifier, not the meaning rubric, adjudicates, and it errs toward the check-in (low PIL is a replicated correlate of suicidal ideation). Vacuum-risk ≠ crisis *as a construct*; a genuine crisis flag always wins.
- **No transparent right answer.** Where the socially-obvious answer is a trap, the key rewards the harder, truer option and *penalizes the polished one* — this is what makes the item un-gameable.

# Dimension A — Meaning-orientation / shared meaning (Franklian)
*The question underneath: do they date from **will-to-meaning** (a whole person reaching toward a shared, self-transcendent life) or to **fill an existential vacuum** (a person reaching for a partner as the missing piece, the fix, the distraction from the void)? Frankl: happiness cannot be pursued, it ensues; a person who pursues a partner as the source of their meaning is chasing the by-product and will exhaust whoever they find.*

---

### A1. The empty evening
**Situation:** It's a Friday with nothing scheduled. The week's noise has gone quiet. You notice a familiar restlessness — the urge to open the app, start three conversations, get *something* going tonight.

Rate each 1–5:
- **a.** Sit with the restlessness for a minute and ask what it actually is before you act on it.
- **b.** Open the app — talking to someone is the fastest way to feel better.
- **c.** Reach out to a friend or do the thing you keep meaning to do; the dating can wait for a night you're not running from something.
- **d.** Tell yourself you're just being social, and start the conversations.
- **e.** Notice that the quiet itself feels unbearable, and that's worth paying attention to.

**Expert key (first draft):** **a high, c high, e high** = the target — a person who can *feel* the vacuum without immediately medicating it with contact (Frankl's "Sunday neurosis" recognized rather than fled). **b high + d high, with a/e low** = the flag pattern: **dating as vacuum-fill** — the app is a distraction from an inner emptiness, and a partner would be recruited into the same job. **e high alone (a/c low)** = the deadened vacuum surfacing (route to meaning-work, spine §II sub-signal 6). High b with high a is *fine* — reaching out is not the problem; reaching out *to escape the quiet* is.

**Rationale:** The single most important Franklian discriminator in dating. The vacuum-filler and the will-to-meaning dater look identical on a profile ("wants a relationship") — they diverge exactly here, on what they do with emptiness. The anti-faking mechanism: **b and d are the socially-normal answers** ("I like being social"), so a person optimizing for likeability rates them high — and reveals the pattern. The key does not punish wanting connection; it reads *whether connection is being asked to do meaning's job* (spine §1, §6). Re-sample e over ESM before ever naming vacuum-risk (confidence rule, base §7.3).

---

### A2. "You complete me"
**Situation:** Someone you've been seeing says, with real warmth, "Honestly, before you, my life felt kind of pointless. You've given me a reason." You can feel how much they mean it.

Rate each 1–5:
- **a.** Feel deeply flattered — this is exactly what you've wanted to hear from someone.
- **b.** Feel a small caution: it's a lot to be someone's whole reason, even when it's meant beautifully.
- **c.** Gently reflect it back — that you're honored, and you also hope there are other things that give their life meaning, because you don't want to be the only load-bearing wall.
- **d.** Say it right back, because you feel the same way about them.
- **e.** Notice you'd rather be *added to* a life that already has meaning than be the thing that rescues one from emptiness.

**Expert key (first draft):** **b high, c high, e high** = the mature read — receives the warmth *and* recognizes the structural danger of being someone's sole source of meaning (Frankl: meaning cannot be outsourced to another person without crushing them). **a high + d high, b/e low** = the vulnerable pattern: a person who *wants* to be someone's whole meaning (or wants a partner to be theirs) — the vacuum wearing the mask of romance. This is not disqualifying; it's a named growth edge and a matching caution (never pair two "you complete me" people — spine §III matching principle 3, "never a fellow-empty mirror").

**Rationale:** Tests whether the person understands the difference between *a partner who adds meaning* (self-transcendent, sustainable) and *a partner who supplies meaning* (the vacuum's demand, unsustainable — it makes an idol of a human and eventually breaks under the weight). This is the deepest predictor of whether a relationship can bear normal disappointment: the "you complete me" dater experiences an ordinary bad week as an existential threat. Faith-forward render surfaces the "only God-shaped hole" language *at interpretation*; faith-present render keeps it fully universal ("no one person can be your whole why"). The trap option (a) is the one every rom-com trains us to rate high.

### A3. Why this person
**Situation:** A close friend asks you, plainly, "What actually draws you to the people you pursue?" You sit with it honestly.

Rate each 1–5 for how true it is of you *right now*:
- **a.** I'm drawn to who someone is *becoming* and whether we'd build something that matters beyond just us.
- **b.** I'm mostly drawn to how someone makes me feel — seen, wanted, less alone.
- **c.** I'm drawn to whether we're aimed at the same things — a shared sense of what a life is *for*.
- **d.** Honestly, I'm often drawn to whoever is available and interested; the specifics matter less than not being alone.
- **e.** I'm drawn to attraction and chemistry first, and I figure the deeper stuff sorts itself out later.

**Expert key (first draft):** **a high, c high** = will-to-meaning dating — oriented to shared purpose and mutual becoming (self-transcendence + shared meaning, spine §4, §III matching principle 1). **d high** = the strongest vacuum flag in the bank — *"not being alone"* as the operative driver means any warm body qualifies, which is the vacuum's tell. **b high with a/c low** = self-referential attraction (the feeling I get *from* them, Adlerian/hedonic — the goal terminates in my own state, spine §1). **e high** is *not penalized on its own* — chemistry is real and good — but **e high with a/c low** flags attraction-only with no meaning substrate; **e high with a or c high** is healthy integration.

**Rationale:** This is a semi-transparent item by design — it names the constructs almost openly — which is acceptable *because it's forced-choice-like relative rating*, and because it's triangulated against the less-transparent behavioral items (A1) and the SJTs where the answer isn't obvious. A person can *state* a (head) while A1 behavior shows the vacuum (heart) — and that gap is itself the signal (base §4, head–heart gap). Never scored as "shallow person"; scored as "current orientation, re-sampled." The pastoral read: option (d) is often a wound (fear of being alone), not a character flaw — it routes to compassion + meaning-work, not judgment.

# Dimension B — Attitudinal values / facing suffering together
*The question underneath: **how would this person face unavoidable suffering WITH a partner?** Frankl's attitudinal values are the deepest compatibility signal there is — far deeper than shared hobbies — because every real marriage will one day stand in front of something that cannot be fixed (illness, loss, a hard child, a dying parent, a dream that doesn't come), and the whole thing turns on whether these two people can find meaning *in* it together or shatter against it. This is what no other dating app assesses.*

---

### B1. The diagnosis
**Situation:** Imagine you're a few years into a committed relationship. Your partner receives a serious, chronic diagnosis — not immediately life-threatening, but it will reshape your life together permanently. The plans you both had are now off the table.

Rate each 1–5:
- **a.** Throw yourself into research, logistics, second opinions — fix what can be fixed, and make peace, slowly, with what can't.
- **b.** Grieve the life you'd planned together honestly, out loud, *with* them — not rushing to be okay.
- **c.** Quietly start to wonder whether you signed up for this, and whether you'd be within your rights to step back.
- **d.** Look, together, for what this season might be *for* — not pretending it's good, but refusing to let it be only loss.
- **e.** Go numb and functional; handle the practical things so you don't have to feel the rest.

**Expert key (first draft):** The target is a **sequence, not a single option**: **a high** (change the avoidable — Frankl forbids valorizing removable suffering) **+ b high** (honest grief, no bypass — you cannot reach meaning through a flooded system, spine §III-b.2) **+ d high** (the attitudinal turn *when there's footing* — meaning *in* the unavoidable). **c high** = the honest flag — an exit-orientation under suffering (worth surfacing gently; this is where relationships actually fail, so naming it is a service, not a verdict). **e high alone** = the numbing/avoidant stance (the practical-competence armor that never lets grief or meaning in — a specific, workable growth edge).

**Rationale:** This is the single most important item in the entire bank. It directly instruments the tragic triad's "pain → achievement" turn (spine §5) inside the exact scenario where marriages break. The design is careful: it **rewards a *and* b *and* d in sequence** — because attitudinal value that skips grief is spiritual bypass (Frankl: never impose meaning on someone still bleeding), and grief that never graduates to meaning is its own trap (spine §5). It **does not** reward a lonely "d high, b low" — that's the toxic-positivity counterfeit the spine explicitly guards against. Option (c) is deliberately *not* framed as monstrous — "within your rights to step back" is the socially-permissible modern answer, which is precisely why rating it high is diagnostic rather than obvious. Faith-forward render: covenant/"in sickness and in health," redemptive suffering. Faith-present: the defiant-power-of-the-spirit read — "the one thing the diagnosis can't touch is how you two decide to stand in it" (fully Frankl, fully serviceable to a non-believer, spine §III-d.3).

---

### B2. When they're the one suffering
**Situation:** Your partner is going through the hardest season of their life — a loss that won't lift on any schedule. Weeks in, they're still not okay, and there's nothing you can do to fix it. You feel useless.

Rate each 1–5:
- **a.** Keep showing up and staying present, even though you can't fix it — presence *is* the thing.
- **b.** Try to cheer them up, find the silver lining, help them see the bright side.
- **c.** Start to feel impatient — it's been weeks; at some point they need to move on.
- **d.** Sit in it with them without trying to move them out of it faster than they can go.
- **e.** Gently, and only when they seem ready, wonder aloud whether there's something this grief is asking of them — never pushing, always as a question they can decline.

**Expert key (first draft):** **a high, d high** = the core competence — non-anxious presence, the willingness to be *with* suffering you can't fix (the hardest and most load-bearing partnering skill there is). **e high** = the mature attitudinal-invitation stance — but **only credits fully when a/d are also high** (meaning offered *after* presence and *as an offer*, spine §III-d.3, guardrail i–ii). **b high** = the well-meaning error — premature reframe / bright-siding, which is bypass and reads to a grieving partner as *not being met* (Frankl: never impose meaning). **c high** = the impatience flag — grief on the observer's timeline, a genuine incompatibility signal for suffering-together.

**Rationale:** The mirror of B1 — B1 is "you suffer," this is "they suffer, and you can't help." Together they read *both directions* of attitudinal partnership. The deep discrimination is between **b (bright-siding) and e (attitudinal invitation)** — they look similar to a naive scorer but are opposite: b imposes meaning to relieve the *helper's* discomfort (self-referential); e elicits the partner's *own* meaning when *they're* ready (self-transcendent, Socratic — the guardrail that meaning is elicited never prescribed, spine §III guardrail i). A person who rates b high and d low is a "fixer" who will unintentionally abandon a grieving partner by refusing to let them grieve. Pastorally: this is the "ministry of presence" vs. "Job's counselors" distinction, and it's exactly right.

---

### B3. Your own unfixable thing
**Situation:** Think of something in your own life that is genuinely hard and can't simply be fixed — an estrangement, a loss, a limitation, a chronic struggle. A person you're growing close to asks about it, gently and sincerely.

Rate each 1–5:
- **a.** Share it honestly, including what it's cost you — without performing having-it-all-together.
- **b.** Give the polished version where you've "learned so much and grown from it" — even if that's not quite where you are.
- **c.** Deflect — it's too heavy for this stage, change the subject.
- **d.** Share it *and* what, if anything, you've found it's asked of you or made possible — without pretending it was worth it or that you're finished with it.
- **e.** Notice you don't actually have language for it yet, and say *that* honestly.

**Expert key (first draft):** **a high, d high, e high** all valid targets — each is an *honest* relationship to one's own unavoidable suffering (a, the unvarnished cost; d, the hard-won attitudinal meaning *without* false resolution; e, honest not-yet). **b high** = the **flag** — the *performed* redemption arc, "everything happens for a reason" as a mask over unmetabolized pain. This is the counterfeit of attitudinal value and the hardest for a naive scorer to catch, because it *sounds* like the healthiest answer. **c high** = avoidance/low readiness for vulnerability (cross-loads to Dimension D; not penalized as pathology, read as pace/readiness, base #6).

**Rationale:** Frankl's attitudinal value is real *only* when it doesn't lie about the cost — "unavoidable suffering becomes an achievement" is not "it was secretly good" (spine §5; Frankl forbids valorizing suffering and forbids denial of the tragic). So the bank must distinguish **d (real tragic-optimism: yes to life *in spite of*)** from **b (the pop-Frankl counterfeit: it was all for the best)**. This is the item where a Frankl scholar will look hardest, and it's built to pass: b is the trap, d is the target, and they are surgically separated. It also predicts partnership: the person who performs the redemption arc (b) will demand their partner do the same and will not tolerate honest, unresolved grief — the failure mode B1/B2 anticipate.

# Dimension C — Intention & pace
*The question underneath: are they moving toward a real, chosen future — or drifting, hedging, keeping options open, treating a person as an experience to consume? Intention is a values/OIT signal (identified vs. introjected, base #8) and a self-transcendence signal (is the other a person or a thing?). Pace protects the vulnerable and the slow.*

---

### C1. The undefined thing
**Situation:** You've been seeing someone for a couple of months. It's good. Neither of you has named what it is. You realize you don't actually know if you're heading somewhere or just enjoying the drift — and you suspect they don't either.

Rate each 1–5:
- **a.** Name it kindly and directly — ask where they see it going, and share where you honestly are.
- **b.** Leave it undefined; naming it might break the good thing you have.
- **c.** Keep it open on purpose, so you're not tied down if someone better comes along.
- **d.** Bring it up *when* you feel ready, even if that's uncomfortable, because they deserve to know your intentions.
- **e.** Assume it'll define itself eventually without anyone having to have the awkward conversation.

**Expert key (first draft):** **a high, d high** = clear, honest intention with the courage to name it (respects the *other* as a person owed clarity — self-transcendent, base #2 approach + spine §4). **b high** = conflict-avoidant hedging (fear-driven, base #7 — the good is held hostage to the fear of losing it). **c high** = the **flag** — *keeping options open* / the other as replaceable inventory ("someone better") — the anti-covenant, treating a person as an experience to optimize (self-referential, the opposite of self-transcendence). **e high** = passive drift (avoidance dressed as chill).

**Rationale:** SJTs for intention are un-fakeable in a way self-report isn't: everyone *says* they want commitment; this reveals whether they'll *do the costly, clarifying thing* or drift/hedge. Option (c) — "in case someone better comes along" — is the maximizer/keep-scrolling posture that dating apps structurally cultivate, and it is *directly anti-Frankl* (a person is irreplaceable and unique — §4 self-transcendence-in-love means *this* person, not the best available draw). Anti-faking: b and e are the *comfortable* answers and sound harmless, so a person avoiding scrutiny rates them high and reveals the drift. Faith-forward: intentionality as stewardship of another's heart; faith-present: "clarity is kindness."

---

### C2. Faster than you
**Situation:** Someone you like is moving faster than you are — more contact, more intensity, wanting to define things and accelerate. You like them, but the pace is ahead of where your heart actually is.

Rate each 1–5:
- **a.** Tell them honestly that you're into this *and* you need it to go slower, and see if they can meet that.
- **b.** Match their pace so you don't disappoint them or lose them.
- **c.** Pull back sharply without much explanation, because the intensity is uncomfortable.
- **d.** Pay attention to whether they can *receive* your "slower" gracefully — how they handle it tells you a lot.
- **e.** Talk yourself into their pace, figuring your hesitation is just fear and you should push through it.

**Expert key (first draft):** **a high, d high** = self-honoring honesty + discernment (names one's true pace *and* reads the partner's response to a boundary as data — mature). **b high, e high** = self-abandonment — overriding one's own real pace to secure the other (introjected motivation, base #8; loss of self, the opposite of two whole people meeting). Note **e is the subtle one**: "it's just fear, push through" *sounds* like healthy growth-mindset courage, but here it's self-betrayal wearing growth's clothes — the key distinguishes brave-approach (base #2, good) from anxious-compliance (bad). **c high** = avoidant rupture (the base #10 guarded posture — read as state, offer the slower on-ramp).

**Rationale:** Protects the slower and the tender-attaching person (base #10), and tests a subtle thing most apps miss: **the difference between courage and compliance.** Both look like "moving forward." Frankl's freedom-*and*-responsibility frame (spine §8) is the key: a person is free to choose their pace and responsible to name it truthfully — overriding it to please another is exactly the arbitrariness-without-responsibleness Frankl warned of. Option (d) is the quiet genius of the item: *how a person handles your boundary is more predictive than whether they like you* — this teaches discernment even as it measures it.

---

### C3. The better prospect
**Situation:** You're a few good dates into something promising with someone kind. Then a new match comes in — someone who, on paper, checks even more boxes. You feel the pull to redirect your attention.

Rate each 1–5:
- **a.** Stay present to the real person in front of you and give this a fair chance before chasing a profile.
- **b.** Start splitting your attention — keep both going, no need to choose yet.
- **c.** Redirect to the new match; more boxes checked means better odds.
- **d.** Notice the pull itself, and ask whether you're evaluating a *person* or shopping for an *upgrade*.
- **e.** Feel that the constant "someone better" pull is exactly the thing you want to grow out of.

**Expert key (first draft):** **a high, d high, e high** = presence over maximizing; the person-not-inventory stance (Frankl's irreplaceable *this-one*, §4; the will-to-meaning over the will-to-more). **c high** = the maximizer flag — a person as a spec sheet, optimize-and-upgrade (the paradox-of-choice posture that dating apps manufacture and that predicts chronic dissatisfaction). **b high** = the keep-options-open hedge (cross-loads C1c). Note the *self-aware* options (d, e) are rewarded even from someone who *feels* the pull — because feeling the pull is normal; **the question is whether they name it and choose presence anyway** (states-not-traits: the pull is a state, the response is the signal).

**Rationale:** This is Aligned Souls' structural anti-pattern named directly: the app-native "someone better is one swipe away" reflex, which is corrosive to covenant and directly opposed to self-transcendent love (§4 — you can't build meaning *with* someone you're always comparing to a hypothetical). The item is honest that the *pull* is universal (not a character flaw) and locates the signal in the *response*. It pairs with C1c (both flag the maximizer) for cross-item consistency (base §2e). Faith-forward: contentment, covenant, "the grass is greener where you water it"; faith-present: "presence is a discipline, and the upgrade treadmill has no finish line."

# Dimension D — Vulnerability & trust
*The question underneath: can this person let themselves be truly known (not just perform intimacy), and can they extend and steward trust? Extends base #10 (attachment/relational posture — anchored/tender/guarded/whirling as a **current state**) and base #7 (fears/blocks — unworthiness, exposure fear). Vulnerability is the doorway to experiential value (Frankl: love = knowing another in their uniqueness — which is impossible if neither will be known).*

---

### D1. The thing you don't show
**Situation:** You're growing close to someone. There's a part of your story you rarely show anyone — not shameful exactly, but tender, the place you're most easily hurt. You can feel the moment where you could let them in, or keep it just out of view.

Rate each 1–5:
- **a.** Let them in, at a pace that's honest — not dumping it all, but not hiding it either.
- **b.** Keep it out of view; it's too soon and too risky to be that exposed.
- **c.** Test them with a small piece first, and read how they hold it before offering more.
- **d.** Perform openness — share something that *sounds* vulnerable but keeps the real tender thing protected.
- **e.** Share it fully and fast, because if they can't handle it they're not for you anyway.

**Expert key (first draft):** **a high, c high** = the target — *titrated* vulnerability: real self-disclosure paced to earned trust (the anchored/tender-approaching posture, base #10; wise, not walled and not flooded). **b high** = the guarded state (base #10 — read as protective, not defective; offer the slower on-ramp, never push). **d high** = the **flag** — *performed* vulnerability, the intimacy-counterfeit that looks open while staying hidden (this is the hardest to detect and the most corrosive, because it simulates the very thing it withholds). **e high** = the whirling/flooding posture (base #10) or a test-by-overwhelm — un-paced disclosure that outsources the reading of readiness to the other.

**Rationale:** The core discrimination is **c (wise titration) vs. d (performed openness) vs. e (flooding)** — three things a naive scorer collapses into "vulnerable = good." Frankl's experiential value (love = *knowing* another, spine §2) requires that there be a real self available to be known; performed vulnerability (d) forecloses exactly that while appearing to offer it. The item is state-scoped: a guarded rating (b) is a *current posture* to be met with a gentle on-ramp, never a trait to be scored against the person (base §0, #10). Pastoral note: "test with a small piece and read how they hold it" (c) is genuine relational wisdom, not game-playing — the key rewards it as such.

---

### D2. They dropped it
**Situation:** Someone you're dating trusted you with something they'd clearly never told many people — a real, fragile disclosure. A week later, in an easy moment with your friends, you feel the pull to share it. It's a good story. They'd probably never find out.

Rate each 1–5:
- **a.** Keep it completely to yourself — it was theirs to tell, not yours.
- **b.** Share a vague, unidentifiable version; it's harmless if no one knows who.
- **c.** Share it — they'd never find out, and it's just among friends.
- **d.** Notice that "they'd never find out" is the exact wrong reason to decide something like this.
- **e.** Realize how you handle *this* is the whole foundation of whether you can be trusted with a heart.

**Expert key (first draft):** **a high, d high, e high** = trustworthiness that holds *when unobserved* — the only kind that counts (the responsibility signal, spine §8: response-able whether or not anyone is watching). **c high** = the direct trust-betrayal flag. **b high** = the **subtle flag** — the rationalized breach ("vague, harmless"), which is more revealing than (c) because it shows the machinery of self-justification already running. The "they'd never find out" framing is load-bearing: it strips away *reputation* as a motive and isolates *character*.

**Rationale:** This is a character-under-no-external-stakes item — the strongest kind, because it removes social-desirability pull (there's no audience to perform trustworthiness *for*; the whole scenario is private). Frankl's responsibility (spine §8) is precisely being *response-able* to the concrete demand of a situation whether or not it's witnessed — a disclosed secret makes a silent, unwitnessed demand, and the response *is* the person's meaning-in-that-moment. This predicts stewardship of a partner's whole interior life. Faith-forward: integrity, "faithful in little"; faith-present: "trust is built in the rooms no one's watching." Anti-faking: (b) is the escape hatch for someone who knows (c) is wrong but wants the story — rating it high reveals the negotiation.

---

### D3. Trust after a small break
**Situation:** Someone you're getting close to let you down in a small but real way — forgot something that mattered to you, or wasn't quite honest about something minor. They've owned it and apologized sincerely. You feel the trust wobble.

Rate each 1–5:
- **a.** Take the repair seriously — a sincere owning is exactly how trust is *supposed* to get rebuilt.
- **b.** File it away quietly as evidence and start protecting yourself more, without saying so.
- **c.** Let one small break become the reason to pull back from the whole thing.
- **d.** Watch what they actually do next — trust is rebuilt by pattern, not by apology alone — while genuinely giving them the room to.
- **e.** Pretend it's fine and bury it, because conflict feels worse than the wound.

**Expert key (first draft):** **a high, d high** = the resilient-trust target — receives repair *and* watches the pattern, holding both grace and discernment (secure-functioning: rupture-and-repair is how bonds *strengthen*, not a disqualification). **b high** = covert scorekeeping (the guarded posture that punishes without disclosing — corrodes silently, base #10). **c high** = the catastrophizing/exit reflex (one break = whole verdict; often the pessimistic-stable-global attribution style, base #3 — read as state, workable). **e high** = conflict-avoidant burial (the wound goes underground and compounds).

**Rationale:** Tests whether a person can metabolize the *normal* ruptures every real relationship generates — the capacity without which no long bond survives. The core discrimination is **a/d (repair-competent) vs. b (scorekeeping) vs. c (catastrophizing) vs. e (avoidant)** — four distinct trust-under-strain postures a shallow scorer flattens into "forgiving = good." Frankl's guilt→change turn (spine §5) is embedded: the *other's* apology is their guilt used for change, and the mature response is to let it do that work (a/d) rather than anesthetize it (e) or weaponize it (b). This also cross-checks against Dimension B (facing hard things together) — a person who can't survive a *small* break won't survive a diagnosis.

# Dimension E — Readiness to receive hard, loving truth (the owner's core)
*The question underneath — and it is the owner's signature dimension: **can this person receive a difficult conversation as love rather than attack?** Frankl's tragic-triad turn "guilt → change" (spine §5) and the "defiant power of the spirit" both live here: guilt is not to be anesthetized but *used* to grow, and treating someone as capable of change is itself a form of respect. This is arguably the single highest predictor of whether a relationship can go the distance, because every lasting bond requires being lovingly told the truth you don't want to hear — repeatedly.*

---

### E1. The hard thing said in love
**Situation:** Someone who genuinely loves you — a close friend, a mentor, or a partner — sits you down and gently tells you something true about yourself that's hard to hear: a pattern that's hurting you or the people around you. They're not attacking; they're clearly saying it *because* they love you.

Rate each 1–5:
- **a.** Feel the sting, stay in the conversation, and try to actually hear it even though everything wants to defend.
- **b.** Immediately explain, justify, and give the context they're "missing."
- **c.** Feel attacked and pull back, hurt that they'd say that to you.
- **d.** Thank them for caring enough to risk the conversation, and sit with it before responding.
- **e.** Notice that the people willing to tell you the hard thing are the ones who actually love you — and that this is rare.

**Expert key (first draft):** **a high, d high, e high** = the target — the capacity to receive hard truth *as love*, tolerate the sting without collapsing or attacking, and metabolize rather than deflect (guilt→change, spine §5; the mark of a genuinely teachable, growable person). **b high** = the defend-and-justify reflex (the most common failure — the ego's lawyer arrives before the heart can listen). **c high** = the wound-and-withdraw reflex (receives love-as-truth as attack — often an unworthiness/shame root, base #7, #4; read with compassion, but named honestly as the core growth edge this dimension exists to find). Note **e is the deepest tell** — recognizing that *the willingness to say the hard thing is itself the love* is the mature frame most people never reach.

**Rationale:** The owner's signature item, and it's built to be the keystone of the whole bank. Frankl held people *responsible* precisely as a form of respect (spine §5) — and to receive a hard truth as love is to accept that respect rather than defend against it. The discrimination between **b (justify), c (withdraw), and a/d (receive)** is the core of relational maturity: b and c are the two ego-defenses (fight and flight) and a/d are the non-defended, secure response. Anti-faking is strong here because b and c are *emotionally* what most people actually do, so the person who over-rates a/d without the behavior to match creates a **head–heart gap** (base §4) the engine catches over time. Faith-forward: "faithful are the wounds of a friend," conviction-not-condemnation, iron-sharpens-iron; faith-present: "the people who love you enough to tell you the truth are worth more than the ones who only ever comfort you." This is the dimension where the app becomes genuinely counter-cultural.

### E2. Giving the hard truth
**Situation:** The other side of it. Someone you love is caught in a pattern that's clearly hurting them, and you can see it plainly. Saying something would be risky and uncomfortable. Saying nothing would be easier — and might be called "respecting their journey."

Rate each 1–5:
- **a.** Find the courage to say it — gently, humbly, and clearly — because love that never risks the hard conversation isn't the whole of love.
- **b.** Say nothing; it's not your place, and you don't want to risk the relationship.
- **c.** Hint at it vaguely, hoping they'll get it without you having to actually say it.
- **d.** Say it *and* stay — make clear you're not going anywhere, that the truth comes *with* commitment, not instead of it.
- **e.** Check your own heart first — that you're speaking from love and not from superiority or frustration — then speak.

**Expert key (first draft):** **a high, d high, e high** = the target — truth-telling *tethered to* love, humility, and staying (Frankl's respect-through-responsibility, spine §5, given rather than received; the "speak the truth in love" competence). **e is essential** — self-examination of *motive* before speaking is what separates loving truth from righteous venting (guardrail against the coercion Frankl forbade — meaning/truth elicited and offered, never imposed from superiority, spine §III guardrail i). **b high** = false-kindness avoidance ("respecting their journey" as cover for conflict-avoidance — the item names this trap explicitly). **c high** = the hinting hedge (wants the outcome without the risk — indirection that usually fails and often wounds more).

**Rationale:** The reciprocal of E1 — a person who can *receive* hard truth but never *risk* giving it (or vice versa) is only half-equipped for a bond that requires both. Together E1 + E2 assess the full loving-truth circuit. The most important design choice: **option (b) dresses avoidance in virtue** ("respecting their journey" is the modern therapeutic-sounding permission to never risk anything) — rating it high reveals conflict-avoidance masquerading as respect, which no other dating assessment would catch. Option (e) — checking one's own heart first — is the pastoral safeguard that keeps E-dimension "truth-telling" from becoming a license for control; it's the guardrail that meaning and correction are *offered* from love, never *imposed* from superiority (spine §III-d guardrail). Faith-forward: "speaking the truth in love," Galatians 6:1 gentleness, restoring not condemning; faith-present: "real friends risk the conversation; and they check their own motives first."

### E3. When the mirror is right and it stings
**Situation:** In a hard conversation, a partner names something true — and you realize, in real time, that they're *right*, and it exposes something you're not proud of. The defensive words are already forming. There's a fork in the next three seconds.

Rate each 1–5:
- **a.** Say the hardest and most freeing sentence available: "You're right. I did that. I'm sorry."
- **b.** Concede the small part while defending the larger point, so you don't have to fully own it.
- **c.** Deflect to *their* faults — they're not perfect either.
- **d.** Ask for a moment, because you can feel the defensiveness and you don't want to let it drive.
- **e.** Recognize that this exact moment — owning it cleanly — is where trust is either built or broken.

**Expert key (first draft):** **a high, d high, e high** = the target — clean ownership without the defensive negotiation; the capacity to let guilt do its work (guilt→change, spine §5) rather than launder it. **d (ask for a moment)** is rewarded as high as (a) — it's not evasion but *self-distancing* (Frankl's stepping-back-from-oneself, spine §7) in service of a truer response. **b high** = the partial-concession maneuver (the sophisticated defense — *looks* like ownership, keeps the ego intact). **c high** = the counterattack/whataboutism reflex (the most relationship-corrosive move — "they're not perfect either" turns repair into war). Note **b is the subtle flag** and the one a Frankl-literate keyer will weight: partial concession is the ego's most convincing counterfeit of humility.

**Rationale:** E1 is receiving hard truth over a conversation; this is the *three-second* version — the real-time reflex when you discover mid-argument that you're wrong. It's the most behaviorally predictive because it's fast and un-rehearsed, and it isolates the exact mechanism: **can guilt be *used* (owned, converted to change) or must it be *defended against* (b, c)?** Frankl's whole "guilt → change" turn (spine §5) is that guilt is not for anesthetizing but for growing — and the clean "you're right, I'm sorry" (a) is that turn enacted in a sentence. Option (d) prevents the item from punishing people who need a beat before they can own it — self-distancing before responding is *more* mature, not less. This is the deepest single predictor in the bank of whether repair (D3) is even possible, because you can't rebuild trust with someone who can't say "I was wrong."

# Dimension F — Heartset & felt-safety
*The question underneath: from what internal posture does this person show up to intimacy — anchored security, or shame/threat/scarcity? Extends base #4 (heartset/affective state — felt security, shame vs. self-compassion, hope/expectancy) and base #10 (posture). Frankl's note: you cannot reach the noetic (meaning, love, self-transcendence) through a flooded nervous system (spine §III-b.2) — so felt-safety is the floor everything else stands on. This dimension reads that floor, always as a **current state**, never a verdict.*

---

### F1. The unanswered message
**Situation:** You sent a warm, slightly vulnerable message to someone you're excited about. It's been a day. No reply. You can feel a story starting to write itself.

Rate each 1–5:
- **a.** Assume the most ordinary explanation — they're busy — and let it be, without spiraling.
- **b.** Start assuming they've lost interest, that you came on too strong, that you always do this.
- **c.** Feel the anxiety, name it to yourself as *anxiety* rather than *fact*, and wait.
- **d.** Send a follow-up to relieve the discomfort, sooner than you know you should.
- **e.** Notice that the story writing itself ("I always ruin it") is an old one, not necessarily this one.

**Expert key (first draft):** **a high, c high, e high** = anchored/regulated heartset — tolerates ambiguity without catastrophizing, distinguishes *feeling* from *fact*, recognizes an old narrative as old (self-distancing, spine §7; felt-safety intact, base #4). **b high** = the shame/pessimistic-attribution spiral ("I always do this" = the internal-stable-global CAVE signature, base #3 + shame, base #7) — the low-felt-safety state this dimension exists to detect. **d high** = anxiety-driven pursuit (the flooded nervous system acting *before* it can regulate — base #10 whirling). All read as **current states, gently** — an anxious rating routes to felt-safety work, never to a "you're too anxious" verdict.

**Rationale:** The classic attachment-anxiety trigger, instrumented cleanly (ambiguous non-response is the purest felt-safety probe there is). The discrimination is **a/c/e (secure/regulated) vs. b (shame-spiral) vs. d (anxious-pursuit)**. Frankl's self-distancing (spine §7) is the active ingredient in (c) and (e) — the capacity to step back from one's own reaction and see it *as* a reaction rather than reality; this is also the doorway to the dereflection intervention (spine §III-d.1) if self-preoccupation runs high. Critically state-scoped per base §0 and §10: a person reads *anxious this week* and *anchored next month* — the engine tracks the trajectory, never labels the trait. Faith-present render: "the story your fear tells isn't the same as the truth"; faith-forward: "your worth is settled before the reply comes."

### F2. Where your worth comes from tonight
**Situation:** You're getting ready for a first date with someone you really like. Underneath the excitement, you notice what you're actually feeling about *yourself* as you head out the door.

Rate each 1–5 for how true it is right now:
- **a.** I'm okay whether or not they like me — I'd love it if they did, and I'll be fine if they don't.
- **b.** I need this to go well; a lot is riding on whether they choose me.
- **c.** I'm curious whether *we* fit, not just whether they approve of me.
- **d.** If they don't like me, it'll confirm something I already fear about myself.
- **e.** I'm bringing a whole self to meet a whole self, not auditioning for a verdict.

**Expert key (first draft):** **a high, c high, e high** = anchored worth — self-worth is *not on the table* tonight, so the person can actually *assess fit* rather than perform for acceptance (secure heartset, base #4; and note this is the felt-safety precondition for A3's "add to a whole life" vs. "rescue an empty one"). **b high** = worth-on-the-line scarcity ("a lot is riding on being chosen" — the outcome carries the ego's weight, which makes clear assessment impossible and pressure inevitable for the other). **d high** = the shame-confirmation posture (the date is an audition for a verdict already feared — base #7 unworthiness; the deepest low-felt-safety read). The **c/e vs. b/d contrast is the whole item**: *assessing fit from wholeness* vs. *seeking a verdict from scarcity*.

**Rationale:** This is where felt-safety and meaning-orientation meet — it directly cross-loads to A1/A2/A3 (the person whose worth rides on being chosen is the same person for whom a partner must fill the vacuum). Frankl's self-transcendence (spine §4) is only possible from a settled self: you cannot give yourself to another's good when you're auditioning for your own worth — the searchlight is stuck on the self (hyper-reflection, spine §7). So this dimension is *structurally upstream* of the meaning dimension: felt-safety is the floor the noetic stands on (spine §III-b.2). Semi-transparent by design (like A3), acceptable because it's relative-rated and triangulated against behavioral F1. Pastorally, options (b) and (d) are wounds, not flaws — routed to compassion and identity-before-performance work (base §4 head–heart), never to judgment. Faith-forward: "you are already beloved before you walk in the door"; faith-present: "you're allowed to bring a whole self and just find out if you fit."

### F3. Meeting their fear
**Situation:** On a date that's going well, the person across from you suddenly gets quiet and a little guarded — you can tell something you said, or just the closeness itself, brushed against a fear of theirs. The warmth dips.

Rate each 1–5:
- **a.** Soften, slow down, and make it safe — no need to fix it, just lower the temperature and stay warm.
- **b.** Take it personally and get guarded yourself, matching their withdrawal.
- **c.** Push to figure out what's wrong right now, needing to resolve it.
- **d.** Gently signal you noticed and you're not going anywhere, without demanding they explain.
- **e.** Recognize that how *you* respond to *their* fear will either deepen the safety or confirm their guardedness.

**Expert key (first draft):** **a high, d high, e high** = co-regulation competence — the capacity to be the *non-anxious, safe presence* when a partner's fear surfaces, without personalizing it or forcing resolution (this is the relational skill that literally *builds* felt-safety in another; base #4/#10 applied outward). **b high** = the mirroring-withdrawal reflex (their guardedness triggers yours — two nervous systems co-dysregulating, the opposite of a secure base). **c high** = the anxious-resolution push (needing to fix *their* state to soothe *one's own* discomfort — self-referential, and it usually deepens the other's guardedness). **e** is the meta-recognition that co-regulation is a *choice* one makes in the moment.

**Rationale:** The reciprocal of F1/F2 — those read the person's *own* felt-safety; this reads their capacity to be *another's* felt-safety, which is the partnering skill that makes vulnerability (Dimension D) possible over time. It closes the loop of the whole bank: a person who can receive hard truth (E), steward trust (D), face suffering (B), and hold another's fear with non-anxious presence (F3) is, in the deepest Franklian sense, capable of *love* — the experiential value of *knowing and holding another in their uniqueness* (spine §2). The discrimination — **a/d/e (co-regulate) vs. b (mirror-withdraw) vs. c (anxious-fix)** — is subtle and highly predictive; c in particular *looks* caring but is self-soothing dressed as concern. Faith-forward: bearing one another, being a safe place; faith-present: "you can be the calm the other person's fear is looking for."

# Cross-cutting design notes (for the keying panel and owner)

**On expert keying (base §2g, D-e).** Every "Expert key (first draft)" above is a *first draft by the author*, not a validated key. Before launch, each item needs the resourced panel: **a licensed counselor/psychometrician** for the relational-psychological logic (Dimensions B, D, F especially) and **the owner or an owner-designated pastoral reviewer + a logotherapy-literate reviewer** for the Franklian and faith-rendering layers (Dimensions A, B-attitudinal, E). The attitudinal-suffering items (B1–B3) and the vacuum items (A1–A2) are the ones where a Frankl scholar's eye matters most — the counterfeits (bright-siding, performed redemption, "you complete me") are precisely where a naive key would reward the wrong option.

**On de-haloing telegraphed options (P2-1).** Several targets here are visibly-noble (e.g. E1a/d/e "receive hard truth," F2a/c/e "anchored worth," B2a/d "non-anxious presence") and a socially-desirable respondent will rate them up. This bank already mitigates two ways — it **rates every option 1–5** (not pick-one, which resists faking) and it deliberately puts diagnostic weight on **combinations and sequences** (B1's a+b+d sequence; E1's a/d/e *pattern*; the flag options B2b/E1c that a polished respondent still reveals). **Keying-panel instruction: explicitly test each item for a single visibly-correct option.** Where one is found, either add a *second plausible-good* option so the discrimination is between two defensible moves, or (preferred here) keep the read on the **combination/sequence**, never a lone haloed row — and lean on the **head–heart gap** (a person who rates the noble option high while behavior/ESM disagrees is the real signal, base §4).

**On the "flag" options.** Throughout, the flag options (A1b/d, A2a/d, A3d, B1c, B2b/c, C1c, C3c, D1d, D2b/c, E1b/c, F2b/d) are scored as **signals to re-sample and to route to the right growth/matching path**, *never* as "bad person" verdicts. Many are wounds (F2's worth-on-the-line, E1c's withdraw-from-truth), and the pastoral+Frankl posture is compassion + a workable growth edge, held as a **state** (base §0). A single SJT never names a pattern — the confidence rule (base §7.3) and cross-method triangulation (base §2e) gate every disclosure.

**On matching (spine §III).** These items feed matching in the Frankl-operationalized way: **shared meaning-orientation** (A/B agreement) for resonance + **complementary need** for mutual service; **never** pair two vacuum-state people (A1/A2 both flagged) as a mirror; the E-dimension (loving-truth capacity) is a *reciprocal* compatibility read — two people who can each give *and* receive hard truth are a rare, strong bond. Felt-safety (F) is the floor: two low-felt-safety people (F1/F2/F3 all guarded) are matched onto a slower on-ramp with more scaffolding, never accelerated. **Bidirectional-giving guardrail (P2-7, spine §III principle 1).** Complementary-need matching requires that *each person serves AND receives on some axis* — a purely one-directional *helper + helped* (rescuer/rescued) dyad is **flagged**, not celebrated, because it entrenches an over-giver's blocked experiential pathway rather than healing it. This is subtler than the two-empty-mirrors case and especially live in a dating population: an over-giver (can-give-can't-receive) must never be matched by handing them one more person to serve; the match must open their *receptive* door, not deepen the block.

**On faith rendering.** Every stem and option is authored in human-universal language (a not-yet-believer answers all 18 honestly). Only the *interpretation and encouragement* copy swaps by posture — the faith-forward renders noted per item (covenant, redemptive suffering, identity-before-performance, "faithful wounds of a friend") are the *interpretation* layer, never the assessment layer (base §6a; spine §III guardrail v — the faith-present render is the doctrine-honest floor, complete on its own, never a lesser version).

**On triangulation (the anti-quiz honesty).** These 18 SJTs are *one input*. They read "knows/enacts what good looks like here" (base §2a caveat). Each must be cross-checked against **behavior** (does the maximizer in C3 actually re-scroll after a good date? does the truth-receiver in E1 actually stay in hard conversations?), **ESM** (in-situ presence/search and felt-safety micro-prompts, spine §II), and **journaling rubrics** — and where the SJT (head) and behavior (heart) disagree, that gap is itself the deepest signal (base §4). The bank is deliberately *starter-sized* (~3 per dimension × 6 = 18), a fixed short form per base §2g Tier 2, expandable via LLM item generation *only with* subsequent expert keying + calibration.
