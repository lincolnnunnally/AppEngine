## P1 — A Frankl scholar or licensed clinician will catch these

**P1-0. The Aligned Souls SJT deliverable does not exist on disk.** `/scratchpad/` contains the Kindred bank, the spine, the ESM/rubrics, and the voice pack — but **no `aligned-souls` file**. The prompt's "ALIGNED SJT" section is truncated mid-sentence ("...the app is a distraction from") and was never saved. Before anything else: the Aligned Souls item bank is unfinished and unreviewable. Flagging so it isn't assumed complete.

**P1-1. "Existential frustration" is mildly over-clinicalized — and Frankl's healthiest distinction is buried.** Spine §6 (`logotherapy-meaning-spine.md:65`) and the pillars section treat existential frustration almost entirely as pathology-adjacent ("void… breeds the mass neurotic triad"). Frankl is emphatic — and the owner knows this cold — that **existential frustration is in itself neither pathological nor pathogenic; it is often a *healthy* sign** (a human being wrestling with the worth of life, which animals never do). The vacuum is spiritual distress, *not* a disorder; only when it becomes symptomatic is it noogenic neurosis. Your spine gets the *chain* right but never states the redemptive front half. **Fix:** in §6 add one sentence — "Existential frustration is *not* in itself neurotic; Frankl insists it can be a sign of intellectual honesty and depth, not sickness. It becomes noogenic neurosis only when it manifests in clinical symptoms." This is load-bearing because it changes the engine's stance toward the "vacuum in motion" (low-presence/high-search) person from *at-risk* to *awake* — which the docs elsewhere want but here undercut. [Confirmed against the Frankl Institute / *Man's Search* "Logotherapy in a Nutshell."]

**P1-2. The vacuum→crisis firewall is asserted repeatedly but never operationally defined — and that's the one place this can hurt someone.** Across all four docs the rule is "vacuum-risk routes to meaning-work, never to the Safety Gate; the Safety Gate runs first and independently." Philosophically correct (Frankl's anti-psychologism; a meaning problem ≠ a mood problem ≠ a crisis). **But the empirical literature the spine itself cites cuts the other way on risk:** low purpose-in-life (PIL) is a *documented, replicated* correlate of suicidal ideation. A person deep in the deadened vacuum ("none of it matters," "what's the point") is using language that sits **exactly** on the boundary the recall-biased classifier is tuned to over-trigger on. The docs' clean separation ("existential emptiness ≠ crisis") is true *as a construct distinction* but dangerous *as a routing rule* if it's read as "vacuum language bypasses the gate." **Fix (highest-priority clinical one):** state explicitly that the boundary is **asymmetric and gate-first** — the Safety Gate is blind to the vacuum/meaning distinction and evaluates *every* input including vacuum-flagged ones; only inputs the gate *clears* are eligible for meaning-work routing. Add: "'What's the point' is a meaning marker AND a possible risk marker; the classifier, not the meaning rubric, adjudicates, and it errs toward the check-in." Right now `kindred-sjt-item-bank.md:420` and `esm-prompts-and-journal-rubrics.md:7` could be misread by a builder as "vacuum → skip the gate." That must be impossible to misread. (This is your `care-counseling` reviewer's #1 sign-off item.)

**P1-3. Paradoxical intention is scoped correctly but the *deployment* is riskier than the disclaimer admits.** Spine §III-D-2 (`logotherapy-meaning-spine.md:162-164`) and voice §E know paradoxical intention has clinical form and gate it against Safety-Gate flags. Good. But two fidelity problems remain: **(a)** Frankl's paradoxical intention targets *anticipatory anxiety with an involuntary feared symptom* (blushing, sweating, panic, insomnia) — you "cannot will the involuntary on command." The in-app example ("try to make it the most gloriously awkward hello") targets a *social-performance* fear where awkwardness is **partly voluntary and genuinely possible to produce** — so the mechanism ("you can't will it, so it dissolves") *does not cleanly apply*, and a person who "succeeds" at being awkward may confirm the fear rather than dissolve it. This is a real misread of the technique's active ingredient. **Fix:** either (i) re-scope the app version to a truly involuntary target (blushing, voice shaking, mind going blank) so the mechanism holds, or (ii) rename it — call the awkwardness move **"self-distancing via exaggeration/humor"** (which is the *self-distancing* leg of Frankl, legitimately) and stop claiming it's paradoxical intention. **(b)** Paradoxical intention is contraindicated in depression and where humor about the self isn't available (it can read as mockery to a shame-bound person). The gate should include a *low-mood / high-shame* contraindication, not only a Safety-Gate flag. A clinician will flag both instantly.

**P1-4. "Happiness must ensue / cannot be pursued" is slightly conflated with self-actualization-as-side-effect, and the reward-engine claim over-reaches.** Spine §4 (`logotherapy-meaning-spine.md:47-51`) fuses two distinct Frankl claims — (1) self-actualization is a side-effect of self-transcendence, and (2) happiness/success ensue and cannot be pursued — into one move, then draws a strong operational conclusion: "an intrinsic-motivation model that terminates in the self (competence, mastery) is **Adlerian**." That's too strong and a scholar will object. **SDT competence is not Adlerian will-to-power.** Adler's striving is *compensatory* (overcoming felt inferiority, a defensive/agonistic aim); SDT competence is a *non-defensive* basic need for effectance that is perfectly compatible with self-transcendence (one can feel competent *at serving*). Collapsing "self-directed competence = Adlerian" mislabels a well-validated construct and will make the psychologist on your panel wince. **Fix:** soften to — "A reward model whose *terminus* is the self's own standing risks the Adlerian trap; but competence experienced *in the act of self-transcendence* (feeling able to serve well) is Franklian, not Adlerian. Weight self-transcendent reward highest **without** pathologizing competence." The current voice pack (`voice-and-output-copy-pack.md:329-334`, F0 hierarchy) actually gets this balance right — the *spine's* framing is the thing that overclaims and should be brought into line with its own downstream copy.

**P1-5. "Meaning is detected, not invented; found, not assigned" is stated as flat Frankl doctrine — it needs the conscience/perception nuance or a scholar will push.** Spine §1 (`logotherapy-meaning-spine.md:19`) asserts meaning is "detected, not invented." This is *directionally* Frankl (meaning is objective, discovered via conscience, not projected) — but stated baldly it collides with the genuine scholarly debate about Frankl's realism, and more practically it sits in tension with your own repeated, correct rule that meaning is *elicited*. A careful reader asks: if meaning is simply "detected," how is the engine's Socratic elicitation not just helping someone *read off* a pre-existing answer (which risks the very imposition you forbid)? **Fix:** add the missing middle term — **conscience** (Frankl's *Gewissen*, the "meaning organ"). Meaning is discovered *through the person's own conscience perceiving what this situation asks* — which is exactly why the engine can only *elicit* (help the person's own conscience perceive) and never *assign* (tell them the answer). One clause resolves the apparent contradiction and it's the more precise Frankl.

**P1-6. Nietzsche's "He who has a why…" is doing structural work but its status is unmarked.** Spine §III-B (`logotherapy-meaning-spine.md:133`) and the one-line spine (`:176`) build the engagement thesis on *"He who has a why to live for can bear with almost any how"* — attributed parenthetically to "Nietzsche, Frankl's most-cited line." Correct that it's Nietzsche (from *Twilight of the Idols*) and that Frankl quotes it constantly. But the doc leans on it as if it were *Frankl's* thesis, and it is being asked to bear the entire "why keep going" engineering claim. A scholar will want the honest framing: Frankl *adopts* it as a compression of will-to-meaning, but the psychological warrant is Frankl's, not Nietzsche's aphorism. Minor, but this owner cites Nietzsche himself. **Fix:** "(Nietzsche, in *Twilight of the Idols*; Frankl adopts it as shorthand for the will to meaning.)"

**P1-7. K-15's key labels a virtue as a deficit without enough hedging — pastorally live wire.** `kindred-sjt-item-bank.md:344` keys option b ("receiving makes you uncomfortable — you'd rather be the giver") as **"receptive-blocked ▼"** and the rationale (`:350`) calls the "can give, can't receive" pattern "a nameable meaning vulnerability, not a virtue." Franklian and clinically astute — the over-giver *is* a real pathway imbalance. **But** for this owner's population (pastors, dads, caregivers), self-giving is also a genuine *creative-value* strength and often a fruit of real formation, and keying it "away from the healthy pole" risks the engine pathologizing sacrificial love. The item is good; the *key's* framing is too flatly negative. **Fix:** the pole should be **"pathway *imbalance*, not pathway *badness*"** — i.e., don't score creative-giving as ▼; score the *absence of experiential-receiving across many entries* as the imbalance signal. High-b in isolation is a strength; high-b *with* dead experiential markers is the vulnerability. As written, a single high-b rating loads negatively, which will mis-flag your healthiest servants. (This is also a psychometric issue: the construct being scored should be the *ratio/absence*, not the endorsement of giving.)

## P2 — Accurate but under-depth or psychometrically loose where your expertise expects more

**P2-1. SJT keys still telegraph the good answer in several items — the "un-gameable" claim is overstated.** The bank's design intent (key the *hidden clause*, not the surface) is excellent and mostly executed. But specific options are transparently the "right" one and a psychometrically sophisticated respondent (or a socially-desirable one) will rate them up:
- K-01 option **d** ("names the fear *and* moves toward" — explicitly labeled "the gold pattern," `:46`) reads as obviously virtuous.
- K-05 option **a** ("name it to yourself and stay open anyway," `:127`) — textbook defusion, transparently ideal.
- K-16 option **a** ("turn toward someone else," `:363`) — obviously the healthy move.
- K-18 option **e** ("no circumstance can take how you meet it," `:407`) — nakedly the noble answer.
When one option is the visibly correct one, the SJT collapses toward a Likert item wearing a scenario, which is the exact failure mode §2a is built against. **Fix:** for the most telegraphed items, add a *second plausible-good* option so the discrimination is between two defensible moves (approach-with-disclosure vs. approach-with-humor), not good-vs-bad; and/or move the diagnostic weight onto the **combination** (d high *with* e also high = the head–heart tell, which K-01 already does well — extend that pattern). The rating-all-options format helps, but it doesn't rescue an item where one row is haloed. Recommend the keying panel explicitly test each item for a single-haloed-option.

**P2-2. Constructs are conflated in a way the estimator will inherit unless the keying pass separates them.** Several items cross-load *within* a single option, which is different from cross-loading across options and is riskier:
- K-13 option **a** is keyed as *both* "resilient efficacy" (#9) *and* "adaptive attribution / optimistic #3" (`:299`). Those are distinct constructs; a person can have resilient efficacy with an *internal* attribution. Keying one option to two poles of two dimensions means a single rating can't disentangle them — the estimator gets a blurred signal it will falsely sharpen.
- K-08 option **e** loads decisional-balance (#5) *and* "a #11 opening" (`:195`).
- K-11 option **a** loads secure-posture (#10) *and* self-transcendence (#11) (`:255`).
Cross-*item* loading (one scenario feeds two dimensions via *different* options) is the good triangulation design. Cross-*option* loading (one option scores two constructs) is construct contamination. **Fix:** the keying panel should split any option that scores two dimensions into the *primary* load, and let the *other* dimension be earned by a *different* option in the same or another item. Flag K-13a, K-08e, K-11a specifically. Note #5 (`kindred-sjt-item-bank.md:418`) already asks for keying confirmation — make this the specific instruction.

**P2-3. "Concurrent-validity anchor" and psychometric targets are named but the design commits to a claim the data can't yet support.** Spine §II (`logotherapy-meaning-spine.md:125`) says "PIL/MLQ items are the concurrent-validity anchor the adapted bank is validated against." Good and honest. But the six sub-signals of #11 are asserted as separately *estimable* (`:107-118`) when the source instruments don't cleanly yield six orthogonal factors — LAP-R's Existential Vacuum subscale is the weakest (α ≈ .69, which the spine honestly reports at `:90`), and "dominant value-pathway" (creative/experiential/attitudinal) has **no validated instrument** behind it at all — the meaning triangle is theoretically central but empirically under-operationalized (there is no psychometrically established "pathway inventory"). So sub-signal 3 is the one riding purely on face validity. **Fix:** mark sub-signal 3 (value-pathway) explicitly as **theory-derived, not instrument-anchored** — its calibration target is construct-coherence and predictive use, not concurrent validity against an existing scale, because none exists. Don't let it inherit the "PIL/MLQ anchor" halo that the presence/search signals legitimately have. This is precisely the "claims beyond evidence" trap the base doc §2f warns against, applied to the spine's own newest construct.

**P2-4. The four-quadrant presence×search model is elegant but the "settled / at rest" quadrant is under-theorized and slightly un-Franklian.** Spine §II sub-signal 2 (`logotherapy-meaning-spine.md:112`, and ESM `:184`) treats *high-presence/low-search* as "settled, at rest — protect it, don't manufacture crisis." Mostly right. But Frankl is subtly uneasy with pure "rest" — the will to meaning is not supposed to *terminate*; tension between what-is and what-ought-to-be (**noö-dynamics**) is, for Frankl, the *healthy* state, and a person with zero search may be either genuinely fulfilled *or* prematurely closed/complacent. The docs collapse both into "protect it." **Fix:** split the low-search/high-presence cell — genuine fulfillment (protect) vs. *complacent closure* (the person who has "arrived" and stopped growing, which Frankl would gently reopen). The disambiguator is whether presence is *dynamic* (still oriented toward tasks ahead) or *static* (nothing left to reach for). This is exactly the depth this owner would add and would notice is missing — Frankl's noö-dynamics term is the missing scholarship.

**P2-5. Dereflection's mechanism is stated slightly too mechanically.** Spine §7 (`logotherapy-meaning-spine.md:73`) and voice §E are good, but the framing "dereflection cures by self-forgetting, which is the doorway to self-transcendence" slightly instrumentalizes it. Frankl's dereflection isn't primarily "distract yourself outward"; it's the restoration of the person's **capacity for self-transcendence that hyper-reflection had blocked** — the outward turn is toward a *concretely meaningful* task/person, not just *any* external object. The voice copy (`voice-and-output-copy-pack.md:295`, "who could use a two-line note") is fine but risks reading as "do a distraction task." **Fix:** one clause — the outward turn must be toward something the person *finds meaningful*, not merely external; dereflection is meaning-substitution, not attention-diversion. Otherwise a builder implements "go text someone" as a fidget, which is not the technique.

**P2-6. The head–heart gap leans on dual-process theory as if settled; a psychologist will want the seam acknowledged.** The base doc (§4) and the rubrics (`esm-prompts-and-journal-rubrics.md:255-277`) map head/heart cleanly onto propositional/associative (System 2 / System 1). It's a *useful* frame, but "head vs. heart" as stated belief vs. *enacted/felt* belief is actually **three** things being fused: (1) explicit vs. implicit *cognition* (dual-process), (2) stated vs. *behavioral* enactment (which is a values-action gap, an ACT/behavioral construct, not dual-process), and (3) *felt security* (affect/polyvagal). Rubric D (`:264-269`) actually scores all three under one "gap" — which is defensible operationally but theoretically muddy. This owner (psych degree) will notice the head/heart binary is carrying three distinct constructs. **Fix:** acknowledge in one sentence that the "gap" is a *composite* (implicit-cognition + behavioral-enactment + felt-affect) and that the three can diverge from each other, not only from the "head" — e.g., someone can *behave* worthy while *feeling* unworthy (enactment ahead of affect), which is a *different* and hopeful gap the current model would misread as "aligned." That's a real missing case.

**P2-7. Matching on "complementary need / mutual service" is Franklian but risks engineering a rescuer/rescued dyad.** Spine §III-C matching principle 1 (`logotherapy-meaning-spine.md:150`) — "shared meaning bonds; complementary need generates it… each becomes the other's occasion to give." Beautiful and genuinely Frankl. **But** clinically, "one has a gift the other needs" is one keystroke from a **codependent / caretaker-and-project** pairing, especially in a population defined by loneliness and can't-receive over-givers (your own K-15 profile). The docs guard the *vacuum-mirror* case (principle 3, correctly) but not the *asymmetric-service* case, which is subtler and more common. **Fix:** add a guardrail — complementary-need matching requires *bidirectional* giving (each serves *and* receives on some axis); a purely one-directional "helper + helped" match is flagged, because it entrenches the over-giver's blocked experiential pathway (the exact §II sub-signal 3 vulnerability) rather than healing it. Right now the matching layer could *worsen* the very profile K-15 diagnoses.

## P3 — Polish (a careful reader notices; not wrong)

**P3-1. "Third Viennese School" is correct but the ordering claim is imprecise.** Spine §1 (`logotherapy-meaning-spine.md:15`) — fine as written. Minor: Frankl himself was careful that logotherapy *supplements* rather than *supersedes* psychoanalysis/individual psychology. One word ("after" → "alongside/after") avoids implying replacement, which the rest of the doc (one-substrate, integrative) actually intends.

**P3-2. Voice pack F1 self-transcendent reward can slide toward the thing it warns against.** `voice-and-output-copy-pack.md:339` — "*Hold onto how that felt. That feeling is the real reward.*" This subtly re-centers the *doer's feeling* as the reward, which is the by-product-chasing §4 warns against. Frankl would say the *serving* was the reward; the good feeling *ensued* and shouldn't be re-grasped. Tiny tension between the copy and its own thesis. **Fix:** "That mattered — not because of how it made you feel, but because it was real for {who_it_served}. The good feeling is the echo, not the aim." Keeps the ensue-don't-pursue logic intact even in the reward line.

**P3-3. The "defiant power of the spirit" language is used well but appears in three docs with slightly different glosses.** `logotherapy-meaning-spine.md:41`, `kindred-sjt-item-bank.md:407` (K-18e), `voice-and-output-copy-pack.md:255` — all good, all faithful. Just ensure the *Trotzmacht des Geistes* gloss is identical across them ("the human spirit's capacity to defy its own psychophysical conditions") so the owner's terminology stays stable across the build. Currently the spine says "take a stand against one's own conditions," the SJT says "noetic freedom intact under suffering," the voice says "the last, unkillable freedom" — all true, but a terminology pass would unify them.

**P3-4. ESM-16 / ESM-18 optional lines feed the rubric but the Safety-Gate ordering note should be inline, not only in the header.** `esm-prompts-and-journal-rubrics.md:124,136` — the `[tap + optional line]` prompts on *suffering* and *emptiness* ("it just hurt," "empty") are exactly where a distress disclosure can arrive. The doc's global "Safety Gate first" inheritance covers it, but for a build these two specific prompts should carry the gate reminder *at the item*, since they're the highest-risk free-text surfaces in the whole ESM set. One-line note per item.

**P3-5. "Live as if for the second time" is rendered well but only in the transitoriness corner.** Spine §8 (`logotherapy-meaning-spine.md:80`) and voice §D2 tie the categorical imperative to transitoriness/responsibility — correct. It's slightly under-used: it's arguably the single most actionable Frankl line for a *growth* app (it converts "someday" into "this rep, now") and deserves a place in the *next-step* framing (voice §B), not only the suffering module. Opportunity, not error.

## What is genuinely right (so the owner doesn't over-correct)

So the fix-list isn't read as "it's all broken" — these are done *well* and should not be touched:
- **Self-transcendence as the hinge** (spine §4) and its correct consequence that reward must weight serving-others highest — the central Frankl move, gotten right.
- **Attitudinal values hard-bounded to *unavoidable* suffering**, with explicit "never valorize avoidable suffering / change it instead" (spine §5, §III-D-3; K-18 boundary; voice D0). This is *the* place pop-Frankl and spiritual-bypass live, and the docs are rigorous here.
- **The sequenced soothe-then-meaning gate** (voice §D0) — clinically correct; you cannot reach the noetic through a flooded nervous system. The forbidden-strings list ("everything happens for a reason," "God won't give you more than you can handle," "at least…") is exactly right and pastorally mature.
- **Faith-present as the doctrine-honest floor, not a watering-down**, grounded in noetic ≠ religious (spine §3, guardrail v). This genuinely serves the not-yet-believer without coercion — it matches Frankl's own stance and will satisfy the pastoral reviewer.
- **Meaning elicited, never prescribed / "the counselor must not impose"** (spine §III-D guardrail i; voice invariant 2) — the anti-imposition rule is the thing amateurs miss, and it's threaded through every doc.
- **Presence×search as two axes with the SONG↑/PIL↓ "vacuum in motion" as the highest-yield reachable state** (spine §II; ESM read-rules) — this is the most sophisticated single design decision in the whole set and it's correct.
- **K-14's "reconstruct a felt-good week" instead of "what gives your life meaning?"** — excellent instrument design; the cold direct question is unanswerable and fakeable, and the reconstruction reveals pathway far less gameably.
- **K-17d honoring anti-bypass resistance as *healthy*** rather than something to override — genuinely wise; most designers would key resistance as a deficit.
