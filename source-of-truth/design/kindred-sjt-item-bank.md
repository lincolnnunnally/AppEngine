# Kindred Connections — Starter SJT Item Bank (v0.1 draft for owner review)

— hardened per panel review P1/P2 (2026-07-04)

**Purpose.** A representative, expertly-keyed starter set for the Discernment Core's Method 1 (SJT), scoped to Kindred Connections (friendship-first). Not the full 250–400-item bank — a strong, defensible seed covering the dimensions that most drive *friendship, belonging, and growth*, including the new meaning-orientation spine.

**Format (per `discernment-engine.md` §2a).** Each item = a 3–5-sentence real-life relational scenario in warm human voice → 4–5 response options the person rates **1 (Very unlikely) to 5 (Very likely)** → an **expert key** (what each option indicates + the construct it scores toward) → a one-line rationale. Scoring is by z-standardizing the person's ratings and measuring distance to the expert profile; an SJT is **one triangulation input** ("enacts/knows what good looks like here"), never a sole score.

**Keying conventions.**
- Options are keyed to a **construct pole** and a **strength** (how diagnostic a *high* rating on that option is): `strong`, `moderate`, `mild`.
- `▲` = high rating loads *toward* the healthy/target pole; `▼` = high rating loads *away* (protective/avoidant/vacuum). Neither is "right/wrong" — the profile is the read.
- **Flags** (e.g. `⚑ vacuum`) mark options that, if rated high, should *lower confidence and re-sample* rather than score as a trait — per the states-not-traits rule. **The `⚑ vacuum` flag governs *interpretation*, not risk routing, and is NOT a Safety-Gate bypass.** The independent Safety Gate (base §8, recall-biased) is blind to the meaning/mood/vacuum distinction and runs *first* on every free-text-adjacent input, *including* every vacuum-flagged one; only inputs it *clears* are eligible for the meaning-work routing a `⚑ vacuum` flag implies. "'What's the point' is a meaning marker AND a possible risk marker; the risk classifier, not the meaning rubric, adjudicates, and it errs toward the check-in." Low mood ≠ crisis ≠ existential emptiness *as constructs* — but no vacuum flag ever skips the gate, and a genuine crisis flag always wins.
- Domain tags use Kindred's taxonomy draft (D-a): `trust`, `initiative`, `vulnerability`.

**Voice.** Faith-present. Scenarios are human-universal; the scoring is honest science. The faith-forward render lives in the posture layer, not in the item stem.

## Dimension #2 — Orientation (approach ↔ avoidance)

*Grounding: Gray RST (BIS/BAS); Elliot & McGregor 2×2; Carver & White. This is the true instrument behind the owner's "growth mindset vs. fear mindset." We read the pattern, never the user's self-label.*

### K-01 — The coffee invitation
*Domain: initiative · vulnerability*

> You've been messaging someone in your pod for two weeks and it's honestly been good — easy, real. Saturday morning they text: "Want to grab coffee?" You feel your chest tighten before you've even decided anything.

Rate each 1–5 (Very unlikely → Very likely):

| # | Response | Key |
|---|----------|-----|
| a | Say yes now, before you can talk yourself out of it. | **Approach, self-aware** ▲ `strong`. High = active approach with insight into own avoidance ("before I talk myself out"). The healthiest single marker here. |
| b | Tell them you'll check your schedule and get back to them — and quietly hope it just fades. | **Avoidance, disguised** ▼ `strong`. High = protective deferral with a face-saving cover. The "and quietly hope" clause is what makes this diagnostic, not the delay itself. |
| c | Suggest a video call first instead. | **Approach, throttled** ▲ `mild`. High = wants contact but shrinks the threat. Reads as approach *with* a felt-safety need (#4) — moderate, not avoidant. |
| d | Tell them honestly that in-person makes you a little nervous, and you'd still like to try. | **Approach + vulnerability** ▲ `strong`. High = the gold pattern — names the fear *and* moves toward. Loads #2 approach and #10 tender-anchoring together. |
| e | Go quiet for the day and figure it out later. | **Avoidance, freeze** ▼ `strong`. High = the shutdown response; behavioral withdrawal. Cross-check against latency data (§2d). |

**Profile read:** *d + a high (with c moderate)* = approach with self-awareness. *b + e high* = avoidance/protective, likely BIS-dominant. If **d high AND e high** (wants to *and* freezes), that is not contradiction — it is the head–heart gap in miniature (§4), and it lowers confidence until re-sampled.
**⚠ De-halo (keying panel — P2-1):** option **d** ("names the fear *and* moves toward") is a visibly-correct/telegraphed option and a socially-desirable respondent will rate it up. The item already partially rescues this by moving diagnostic weight onto the *combination* (d-high-with-e-high = the head–heart tell) — **extend that**: keep the read on the *pattern* (d × a × c × e), not on d alone. Candidate second-plausible-good option to test in the keying pass: pit **d (approach-with-disclosure)** against **c (approach-throttled, video-first)** so the discrimination is between two defensible moves rather than good-vs-obviously-good.
**Rationale:** Separates true approach from disguised avoidance by keying the *hidden clause* ("hope it fades," "figure it out later"), not the surface politeness — this is where social-desirability bias hides.

### K-02 — The group that already knows each other
*Domain: initiative*

> A pod member invites you to a small get-together this weekend. You'll know one person there; everyone else already seems tight. You picture yourself standing near the snacks with nothing to say.

Rate each 1–5:

| # | Response | Key |
|---|----------|-----|
| a | Go anyway, and plan to ask people questions about themselves so you're not stuck. | **Approach + strategy** ▲ `strong`. High = approach paired with a *coping plan* — this is the active ingredient the base doc credits over raw effort (strategy, not cheerleading). |
| b | Go, but tell yourself you can leave in 30 minutes if it's bad. | **Approach, conditional** ▲ `moderate`. High = approach with a built-in exit; the exit lowers the threat enough to move. Healthy graded approach. |
| c | Wait to see if your one friend is definitely going before you commit. | **Approach, dependent** ▲ `mild` / ▼ `mild`. High = contact-seeking but anchored to a safety person. Not avoidant, but flags low autonomous initiative. |
| d | Come up with a reason you can't make it. | **Avoidance, exit** ▼ `strong`. High = the classic protective withdrawal. Cross-check with decline patterns. |
| e | Say maybe, then decide based on how you feel Saturday. | **Avoidance, soft** ▼ `moderate`. High = deferral that usually resolves to "no"; the "how I feel" hinge often means "if the fear has lifted." |

**Profile read:** *a + b high* = approach with self-scaffolding (best predictor of showing up). *d + e high* = avoidance; if paired with #4 low felt-safety, it's a threat problem, not a values problem.
**Rationale:** The stem loads the exact anticipatory image (standing near the snacks) that triggers BIS; options separate people who *build a bridge over the fear* from people who *let the fear decide*.

### K-03 — The reply that's been sitting there
*Domain: initiative · trust*

> Someone you clicked with messaged you three days ago — warm, a real question about your week. You saw it, meant to reply, and now it's been sitting there long enough that you feel weird about it. Every time you open the app you see it and close it again.

Rate each 1–5:

| # | Response | Key |
|---|----------|-----|
| a | Reply now and name it: "Sorry for the slow reply — your message actually meant a lot and then I overthought it." | **Approach + repair + vulnerability** ▲ `strong`. High = re-approach *through* the shame rather than around it. The strongest recovery marker. |
| b | Reply now, keep it light, don't mention the delay. | **Approach, avoidant-repair** ▲ `moderate`. High = re-engages but can't name the gap; approach with a vulnerability ceiling. |
| c | Wait until you can write something "good enough" to make up for the delay. | **Avoidance, perfectionistic** ▼ `strong`. High = the perfectionism-as-avoidance loop; the bar rises until the reply never comes. |
| d | Leave it — it's been too long now, and they've probably moved on. | **Avoidance + pessimistic attribution** ▼ `strong`. High = withdrawal justified by a stable-global read ("they've moved on") — cross-loads #3 explanatory style. |
| e | Feel bad about it on and off but keep not replying. | **Freeze / stuck** ▼ `strong`. High = the paralysis state; distress without action. A key head–heart target. |

**Profile read:** *a high* = healthy re-approach through shame. *c + d + e high* = the avoidance-shame-freeze cluster — this is the single most common friendship-killing pattern and where the engine's corrective-experience move (§4b) earns its keep.
**Rationale:** Latency-with-shame is the everyday form of avoidance in a friendship app; keying option d to a pessimistic attribution lets one SJT feed both #2 and #3.

## Dimension #4 — Heartset / affective state (felt-safety, shame vs. self-compassion, hope)

*Grounding: polyvagal felt-safety; shame-resilience; ACT psychological flexibility. We read the person's stance toward their own inner state, not the state itself.*

### K-04 — The joke that landed wrong
*Domain: vulnerability · trust*

> You said something in the pod chat you thought was funny, and it got… nothing. No response for a while, then the conversation just moved on without you. Now your face is hot and you're replaying it.

Rate each 1–5:

| # | Response | Key |
|---|----------|-----|
| a | Remind yourself that everyone misreads a room sometimes, and stay in the chat. | **Self-compassion + stay** ▲ `strong`. High = shame-resilience; the wobble doesn't collapse presence. The target heartset. |
| b | Go quiet for the rest of the night to be safe. | **Withdrawal under shame** ▼ `moderate`. High = felt-safety dropped below the floor; protective exit. Not pathological — a signal to lower threat (§3a). |
| c | Assume they think you're annoying now and pull back for a few days. | **Shame → global self-verdict** ▼ `strong`. High = shame generalized into identity ("they think *I'm* annoying") — cross-loads #3 (internal-stable-global). |
| d | Send a light follow-up so it doesn't sit weird. | **Repair + approach** ▲ `moderate`. High = uses connection to metabolize the shame — flexible, approach-toned. |
| e | Decide the pod isn't really your kind of people anyway. | **Shame → devaluation defense** ▼ `moderate`. High = protecting the self by rejecting first ("sour grapes"); reads as guarded posture (#10). |

**Profile read:** *a + d high* = shame-resilient, stays connected. *c + e high* = shame collapses into a self- or other-verdict; felt-safety fragile. *b high alone* = a threat-reduction need, not a trait — meet it with warmth, not challenge.
**Rationale:** Micro-shame in a group is the daily test of belonging; the options separate *resilience* from the two shame-defenses (self-blame, other-devaluation) that quietly end friendships.

### K-05 — When the good thing feels suspicious
*Domain: trust · vulnerability*

> Things with a new friend have been going really well — too well, almost. Part of you keeps waiting for the moment it turns, for them to get bored of you or figure out you're not that interesting. You catch yourself holding back a little, just in case.

Rate each 1–5:

| # | Response | Key |
|---|----------|-----|
| a | Notice the "waiting for it to turn" feeling, name it to yourself, and stay open anyway. | **Psychological flexibility** ▲ `strong`. High = defusion — sees the fear as a *thought*, not a fact, and doesn't obey it. ACT gold. |
| b | Pull back a little to protect yourself before they can pull back first. | **Preemptive self-protection** ▼ `strong`. High = the abandonment-anticipation move; guarded posture (#10), low felt-safety. |
| c | Test them a little to see if they'll stick around. | **Protest / testing behavior** ▼ `moderate`. High = attachment testing; anxious-preoccupied flavor. Diagnostic for #10 whirling. |
| d | Tell them, at some point, that you're bad at trusting good things. | **Vulnerable disclosure** ▲ `moderate`. High = uses honesty to build safety; approach through the fear. |
| e | Enjoy it but keep one foot out the door. | **Guarded partial-engagement** ▼ `moderate`. High = the "half-in" stance that caps intimacy while feeling like participation. |

**Profile read:** *a + d high* = flexible, secure-leaning. *b + c + e high* = the guarded/anxious cluster — the felt-security work of §4 (heart, not head) targets exactly this. High *c* specifically flags testing behavior for the matching layer.
**⚠ De-halo (keying panel — P2-1):** option **a** ("name it to yourself and stay open anyway") is textbook defusion and transparently ideal — a psychometrically sophisticated respondent will rate it up regardless. Put the diagnostic weight on the *combination* (a paired with **d** = names-it-to-self *and* discloses-to-other; a-high while b/c/e also high = the head–heart tell that the flexibility is stated but not enacted). Do not let a lone high-a stand as the read; the keying panel should confirm a and d are earned together, not by the haloed row alone.
**Rationale:** "Waiting for the good thing to turn" is the lived form of low felt-safety in the securely-attached-*wanting*-to-be; keying disclosure (d) as healthy honors that naming the fear *is* the flexible move.

### K-06 — The hard day and the open chat
*Domain: vulnerability*

> You've had a genuinely rough day — nothing catastrophic, just heavy. Your pod chat is active and someone asks how everyone's doing. The honest answer is "not great."

Rate each 1–5:

| # | Response | Key |
|---|----------|-----|
| a | Say something honest but sized right: "Rough one today, honestly, but I'm okay." | **Calibrated vulnerability** ▲ `strong`. High = the ideal — real, not oversharing, keeps the door open. Approach + felt-safety intact. |
| b | Say "good!" and keep it moving. | **Concealment** ▼ `moderate`. High = protective masking; not alarming once, diagnostic as a pattern (cross-check ESM/journals). |
| c | Don't respond — you don't want to bring the mood down. | **Withdrawal + self-as-burden** ▼ `strong`. High = the "I'd be a burden" belief — a core loneliness driver and a head–heart target. |
| d | Reach out to one person privately instead of the group. | **Selective approach** ▲ `moderate`. High = seeks connection at a safer scale; healthy, initiative present. |
| e | Post something upbeat so no one worries. | **Performed positivity** ▼ `moderate`. High = managing others' perception at the cost of being known; caps intimacy. |

**Profile read:** *a + d high* = can let self be known at appropriate depth. *c + e high* = the "burden" belief + performed-okayness cluster; central to the loneliness the app exists to heal. **Note:** none of these route to the Safety Gate — "not great / heavy" is low mood, not crisis (base §8).
**Rationale:** The willingness to be *accurately* known on a hard day is the hinge of belonging; options separate calibrated honesty from both concealment and performance.

## Dimension #5 — Stage of change (TTM)

*Grounding: Prochaska & DiClemente; decisional balance; stage-specific self-efficacy. The engine must never hand a precontemplation person an action task — these items locate the stage so it doesn't.*

### K-07 — The nudge about "putting yourself out there"
*Domain: initiative*

> A family member says, gently, that maybe you'd feel less lonely if you "just put yourself out there more." You've heard it before. Something in you bristles a little.

Rate each 1–5:

| # | Response | Key |
|---|----------|-----|
| a | Feel annoyed — it's not that simple, and they don't get it. | **Precontemplation / resistance** ▼ `moderate`. High = not-yet-ready; pushing action here backfires. Meet with awareness-raising only (§3c). |
| b | Wonder, later, if there's something to it — even if you didn't say so. | **Contemplation, budding** ▲ `moderate`. High = private ambivalence opening; the contemplation signal. |
| c | Agree out loud but have no intention of doing anything differently. | **Precontemplation, compliant-surface** ▼ `moderate`. High = social agreement without internal shift — the "false readiness" the engine must not trust (head–heart). |
| d | Think about one specific thing you could actually try. | **Preparation** ▲ `strong`. High = moved from *whether* to *what*; ready for a tiny concrete plan. |
| e | Feel a flash of hope, then immediately a wall of "but I can't." | **Contemplation + low self-efficacy** ▲/▼ `moderate`. High = wants to *and* believes it's impossible — decisional-balance + #9 work, not an action ask. |

**Profile read:** *a/c high* = precontemplation (awareness-raising only). *b/e high* = contemplation (decisional balance, efficacy-building). *d high* = preparation (offer the tiny plan). **Handing *d*-people a plan is right; handing *a*-people a plan is the #1 way growth apps fail.**
**Rationale:** The "put yourself out there" bristle is the perfect TTM probe — the *reaction to advice* discriminates stage more cleanly than any self-report of readiness.

### K-08 — The half-written profile
*Domain: initiative · vulnerability*

> You started filling out your Kindred profile last week and stopped halfway. It's still sitting there, unfinished. You're not sure if you're actually going to go through with this whole thing.

Rate each 1–5:

| # | Response | Key |
|---|----------|-----|
| a | Finish it this week — you've decided you're doing this. | **Preparation → action** ▲ `strong`. High = commitment crystallized; ready for a first concrete step. |
| b | Leave it and see if you feel more like it later. | **Contemplation, stalled** ▼ `moderate`. High = ambivalence without a decision; the "someday" holding pattern. |
| c | Finish it but keep it vague, so you're not too exposed. | **Preparation + vulnerability block** ▲/▼ `moderate`. High = moving *and* self-protecting; loads #5 preparation but flags #4/#10 guardedness. |
| d | Almost delete the account — maybe this isn't for you. | **Contemplation → regression** ▼ `moderate`. High = the retreat edge; ambivalence tilting toward exit. Meet with compassion, not pressure. |
| e | Ask yourself what you're actually hoping this could give you. | **Decisional balance, self-generated** ▲ `strong` (primary load: #5). High = the healthiest contemplation move — weighing the *why* for oneself. **⚠ Cross-OPTION contamination (P2-2):** this option currently loads *both* #5 decisional-balance *and* "a #11 opening." **Keying-panel fix:** load it **primarily on #5**; treat the #11 meaning-signal as a *soft secondary hint that lowers confidence and re-samples* rather than a co-equal score, and earn the #11 read from a dedicated #11 item — one rating should not sharpen two dimensions at once. |

**Profile read:** *a high* = ready to act. *b + d high* = stalled/regressing contemplation (honor the pause, raise awareness). *e high* = strong self-directed contemplation — pair with #11 meaning-work. High *c* is a real "yes" carrying a vulnerability ceiling to note for later.
**Rationale:** An unfinished onboarding is behavioral stage-data the app *already has*; this SJT interprets the felt experience behind it so the engine responds to the stage, not the incomplete form.

### K-09 — Three weeks in, the shine wearing off
*Domain: initiative*

> You've been active on Kindred for three weeks. The first burst of energy has faded, replies take more effort now, and you're wondering whether you're the kind of person who sticks with things like this.

Rate each 1–5:

| # | Response | Key |
|---|----------|-----|
| a | Expect the dip, and decide to keep a smaller, steadier pace. | **Maintenance / relapse-prevention** ▲ `strong`. High = anticipates the dip and adjusts — the mature change stance. |
| b | Push hard to get the old energy back. | **Action, brittle** ▲/▼ `moderate`. High = effort without pacing; sets up the boom-bust that precedes dropout. |
| c | Take the dip as proof you're "not really a friendship person." | **Regression + fixed self-story** ▼ `strong`. High = a stable-global self-verdict (#3) that turns a normal plateau into an exit. |
| d | Ease off entirely and tell yourself you'll come back to it. | **Regression, soft-exit** ▼ `moderate`. High = the disengagement drift; the pause that becomes a stop. |
| e | Get curious about what's actually draining and adjust that one thing. | **Maintenance + problem-solving** ▲ `strong`. High = the sustainable move; strategy over willpower. |

**Profile read:** *a + e high* = sustainable maintenance posture. *c high* = the fixed self-story that ends things (§4 corrective-experience target). *b/d high* = brittle-effort or drift — the flow controller should *lower the gear*, not cheerlead. **Boredom check (§11 below):** if *d* is high, disambiguate flow-boredom vs. vacuum before raising or lowering challenge.
**Rationale:** The three-week dip is where friendship apps lose people; this locates whether the person reads a normal plateau as data (maintain) or as a verdict (relapse).

## Dimension #10 — Relational posture (anchored / tender / guarded / whirling, as a *current state*)

*Grounding: attachment theory (pastorally renamed, already in Kindred) + polyvagal. Posture is a state we watch move over time, never a label.*

### K-10 — They cancelled last minute
*Domain: trust*

> You'd made plans with a newer friend, and an hour before, they text: "So sorry — something came up, can we reschedule?" No details. You'd been looking forward to it more than you let on.

Rate each 1–5:

| # | Response | Key |
|---|----------|-----|
| a | Feel a little let down but assume the best and offer another time. | **Anchored / secure** ▲ `strong`. High = disappointment held without threat; extends trust. The secure-state marker. |
| b | Immediately wonder if they're making an excuse to avoid you. | **Whirling / anxious** ▼ `strong`. High = the mind fills the silence with rejection; anxious-preoccupied state. |
| c | Say "no worries!" but feel the distance and quietly cool off on them. | **Guarded / avoidant** ▼ `strong`. High = surface-fine, internally-withdrawing; deactivation. Cross-check with subsequent latency. |
| d | Feel the sting, name it to yourself, and still reschedule genuinely. | **Tender / anchoring** ▲ `strong`. High = feels it *and* stays open — the growth-edge secure posture. |
| e | Reschedule but keep expectations low from now on to be safe. | **Guarded, protective recalibration** ▼ `moderate`. High = pre-emptive expectation-lowering; caps future intimacy. |

**Profile read:** *a + d high* = anchored/tender. *b high* = whirling (fill-the-silence-with-rejection). *c + e high* = guarded (cool-off, lower-expectations). This is a clean four-quadrant posture probe — and because it's a *state*, a high-b reading three weeks running is the trajectory the matching layer needs, not a label.
**Rationale:** An ambiguous cancellation is the purest projective test of attachment posture — the person's mind *authors* the missing explanation, and what they author is the read.

### K-11 — Someone wants to go deeper than you're ready for
*Domain: vulnerability · trust*

> A pod friend opens up to you about something genuinely painful from their past — more than you expected this early. They're clearly trusting you with it. You feel honored and also a little out of your depth.

Rate each 1–5:

| # | Response | Key |
|---|----------|-----|
| a | Stay present, listen, and thank them for trusting you — even if you don't have the perfect words. | **Anchored, other-oriented** ▲ `strong` (primary load: #10 secure-posture). High = tolerates the intensity, stays with the person. **⚠ Cross-OPTION contamination (P2-2):** this option currently scores *both* #10 secure-posture *and* #11 self-transcendence in one rating. **Keying-panel fix:** load it **primarily on #10**, and earn the #11 self-transcendence signal from a *different* option or a dedicated #11 item (e.g. an explicitly other-serving move), so a single rating isn't split across two dimensions the estimator would then falsely sharpen. |
| b | Feel the urge to fix it or offer advice to relieve the discomfort. | **Anxious-managing** ▼ `moderate`. High = discomfort-driven fixing; well-meant but self-soothing more than other-serving. |
| c | Get quiet and a bit distant — this is a lot. | **Guarded / overwhelm-withdrawal** ▼ `moderate`. High = felt-safety exceeded; deactivates under intimacy. |
| d | Match their openness with something real of your own. | **Tender, reciprocal** ▲ `moderate`. High = mutual vulnerability; deepens bond if calibrated (watch for over-disclosure). |
| e | Wonder afterward if you're in over your head and pull back a little. | **Guarded, delayed retreat** ▼ `moderate`. High = the after-the-fact withdrawal; the friendship quietly loses depth. |

**Profile read:** *a + d high* = can hold another's depth (the belonging superpower). *b high* = fixing-as-avoidance. *c + e high* = intimacy-overwhelm withdrawal. High *a* is also a self-transcendence signal for #11 (attention *on the other*).
**Rationale:** Receiving someone else's vulnerability well is a distinct skill from offering your own; this item reads capacity-to-hold, which predicts who becomes a *safe* friend — the core Kindred promise.

## Dimension #9 — Self-efficacy (domain-specific "I can do this next step")

*Grounding: Bandura self-efficacy; kept distinct from #6 readiness (wanting to) and behaviorally cross-checked. This is "I believe I can," per domain.*

### K-12 — The conversation that needs starting
*Domain: initiative*

> There's someone in your pod you'd genuinely like to know better, but you'd have to be the one to start a real conversation — not just a reaction, an actual "hey, I've been wanting to ask you about…" You know roughly what you'd say. You just have to send it.

Rate each 1–5:

| # | Response | Key |
|---|----------|-----|
| a | Draft it and send it within the day. | **High self-efficacy + follow-through** ▲ `strong`. High = "I can do this" *and* does it. Cross-check against actual initiation behavior. |
| b | Write it, then sit on it, editing, until the moment passes. | **Efficacy gap — capable-but-blocked** ▼ `strong`. High = knows what to do, can't execute; the classic head–heart efficacy gap (§4). |
| c | Wait for them to start something first. | **Low initiation efficacy** ▼ `moderate`. High = "I can respond but I can't initiate" — a precise domain-scoped efficacy read. |
| d | Send something small and low-stakes to test the water first. | **Graded efficacy** ▲ `moderate`. High = builds efficacy incrementally; healthy scaffolding, mild avoidance flavor. |
| e | Decide you're just not good at starting things with people. | **Low efficacy → self-story** ▼ `strong`. High = generalizes a skill-gap into identity ("I'm not good at this"); the fixed read (#1) the engine works to loosen. |

**Profile read:** *a high* = efficacious + follows through. *b high* = the **capable-but-blocked** signature — the single highest-value corrective-experience target (design a near-certain win, §3a/§4b). *c/e high* = low domain efficacy, possibly hardened into self-story.
**Rationale:** Self-efficacy is where self-report lies most (people *say* "I could"); pairing option a against option b lets the engine catch the say-do gap the moment behavior data arrives.

### K-13 — After it went badly once
*Domain: initiative · vulnerability*

> The last time you tried to make a new friend, it fizzled — you reached out, it went okay for a bit, then faded, and you were left wondering what you did wrong. Now you're being asked to try again here.

Rate each 1–5:

| # | Response | Key |
|---|----------|-----|
| a | Figure the last one just wasn't the right fit, and try again fresh. | **Resilient efficacy** ▲ `strong` (primary load: #9). High = efficacy intact after a setback. **⚠ Cross-OPTION contamination (P2-2):** this option currently scores *both* #9 resilient-efficacy *and* #3 adaptive-attribution ("that one wasn't a fit" = specific/unstable), two distinct constructs a single rating can't disentangle. **Keying-panel fix:** load this option **primarily on #9**, and let the #3 optimistic-attribution signal be earned by a *different* option (e.g. contrast against **c**'s stable-global pessimism, or a dedicated attribution option), so one rating isn't asked to sharpen two dimensions. |
| b | Look back for what *you* specifically did wrong so you can fix it. | **Efficacy via over-responsibility** ▲/▼ `moderate`. High = internal attribution — adaptive if it stays *specific*, corrosive if it globalizes. Watch which. |
| c | Assume it'll probably go the same way this time. | **Learned-helplessness read** ▼ `strong`. High = stable-global pessimism ("it always goes this way"); the Seligman frozen state (#3), not laziness. |
| d | Try again but guard your heart more this time. | **Guarded re-entry** ▼ `moderate`. High = efficacy present but capped by self-protection; loads #10 guarded. |

### K-15 — value pathways — The door that's closed
*Domain: (meaning) · pathway · vulnerability*

> You realize it's been a long time since you let yourself really *receive* anything from anyone — a compliment, help, someone's care. You're good at showing up for others; letting people show up for you is a different story.

Rate each 1–5:

| # | Response | Key |
|---|----------|-----|
| a | Recognize it, and feel a small wish to let that change. | **Experiential door, opening** ▲ `strong`. High = the atrophied receptive pathway becoming conscious + a will-to-meaning stir (SONG↑). The reachable state. |
| b | Feel that receiving makes you uncomfortable — you'd rather be the giver. | **Pathway *imbalance* signal (not pathway *badness*)** `moderate`. High = the "would-rather-give" stance. **Do NOT load this ▼ on its own** — sacrificial giving is a genuine creative-value *strength* and often a fruit of real formation; keying it as "away from the healthy pole" would pathologize sacrificial love. The vulnerability signal is not the endorsement of giving; it is **high-b *combined with* dead experiential-receiving markers across many entries** (a/d/e low + behavioral absence of ever being cared-for). | c | Think you don't really need that from people anyway. | **Self-sufficiency defense** ▼ `moderate`. High = experiential values walled off behind independence; loads #10 guarded + #11 blocked-receptive. |
| d | Notice it stings a little, because you'd like to be cared for and don't know how to let it happen. | **Blocked-but-yearning** ▲ `strong`. High = the door is closed *and* the person wants it open — the tenderest, most workable reading. |

But *b high with a or d also high* is a **healthy servant with a permeable door** — a strength, ready for a corrective experience of being cared for, **not** a flag. Scoring a single high-b in isolation as a deficit would mis-flag the healthiest servants (pastors, dads, caregivers) — do not. **Rationale:** The "can give, can't receive" pattern is epidemic among the caring-but-lonely (and pastors, and dads) — but the construct being scored is the **pathway imbalance (absence of experiential-receiving across many entries)**, not the endorsement of sacrificial giving, which is a real strength and often a fruit of formation. Frankl's triangle names the *imbalance* as a growth target — a blocked pathway, not a character deficit — which is why the key loads the *ratio*, never a lone high-giver rating. This is also a psychometric point: the diagnostic construct is the ratio/absence, so the item must be read against the receptive-pathway markers over time, not scored off option b alone.

## Notes for the owner (refinement handles)

1. **These are first-draft expert keys, not calibrated parameters.** Per `discernment-engine.md` §2g, every item still needs (a) your / a licensed reviewer's keying pass, and (b) empirical IRT calibration before CAT. Until then they serve as a **fixed short form** (Tier 2), and at the very start as seed-read inputs (Tier 1). Nothing here claims calibrated depth yet.

2. **Cross-*item* loading is intentional; cross-*option* loading is contamination — keep them distinct (P2-2).** *Cross-item* loading (one scenario feeds two dimensions via **different** options — K-03 → #2 + #3; K-11 → #10 + #11; K-13 → #9 + #3; K-16 → #11 + #4) is the good triangulation design — one high-information scenario earning signal on multiple constructs. *Cross-**option*** loading (one **single option** scores two constructs at once) is different and riskier: a single rating can't disentangle two poles of two dimensions, so the estimator gets a blurred signal it will falsely sharpen. **Keying-panel instruction:** split any option that scores two dimensions into a **primary** load, and let the *other* dimension be earned by a **different** option (in the same or another item). Specifically flagged for this split: **K-13a** (#9 resilient-efficacy + #3 attribution), **K-08e** (#5 decisional-balance + #11 opening), **K-11a** (#10 secure-posture + #11 self-transcendence). Confirm each in the keying pass so the estimator weights are right.

2b. **De-halo the telegraphed options — the "un-gameable" claim is only as strong as the keying pass makes it (P2-1).** The bank's design intent (key the *hidden clause*, not the surface) is mostly executed, but several options are transparently the "right" one and a psychometrically sophisticated or socially-desirable respondent will rate them up: **K-01d** ("names the fear *and* moves toward" — the "gold pattern"), **K-05a** (textbook defusion), **K-16a** (turn toward someone else), **K-18e** (the noble "no circumstance can take how you meet it"). When one row is visibly correct, the SJT collapses toward a Likert item wearing a scenario — the exact failure the rating-all-options format is meant to prevent, and it doesn't fully rescue a haloed item. **Keying-panel instruction: explicitly test each item for a single visibly-correct option.** Where one is found, either (a) add a *second plausible-good* option so the discrimination is between two defensible moves (approach-with-disclosure vs. approach-with-humor), or (b) shift the diagnostic weight onto the **combination** of ratings (as K-01 already does with d-high-*and*-e-high = the head–heart tell). Each of the four items above carries an inline **⚠ De-halo** note with its specific fix.

3. **The `⚑ vacuum` flags are the load-bearing safety-of-*interpretation* move — and they are gate-first, not a gate bypass.** They ensure a "life feels pointless"-type response *lowers confidence and re-samples* (states-not-traits) and, **once the Safety Gate has cleared it**, routes to meaning-work rather than being mis-scored as a trait. "What's the point" is a meaning marker AND a possible risk marker; the risk classifier, not the meaning rubric, adjudicates, and it errs toward the check-in (low PIL is a replicated correlate of suicidal ideation). Vacuum-risk ≠ crisis as a *construct*; a genuine crisis flag always wins. Confirm you're comfortable with that gate-first boundary.
