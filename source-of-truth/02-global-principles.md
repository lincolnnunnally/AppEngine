# Global Principles

These operational principles apply to AppEngine, generated apps, agent workflows, and future assistant coordination.

## Operational Rules

- Treat people well.
- Remove barriers.
- Help people help themselves.
- Prefer simplicity.
- Respect privacy.
- Respect app boundaries.
- Prevent purpose bleed.
- Build useful things.
- Optimize for transformation over engagement.
- Optimize for people over technology.
- Optimize for stewardship.
- Respect user trust.
- Respect user agency.

## Durable Source

GitHub-visible files, issues, pull requests, and commits are the durable source of truth. ChatGPT and Codex may clarify ideas, but important decisions must move into the repo before agents rely on them.

## Human Stewardship

Lincoln remains the visionary and final steward. Agents reduce repetitive handoff, prompting, testing, and coordination work. They do not replace judgment, approval, or stewardship.

## Life Produces Life

Build systems that help people become stronger and help others. Avoid products that merely collect users, create dependency, or turn people into passive consumers.

Transformation is the product. The app, website, service, workflow, or automation is a tool that removes barriers and helps people move toward life.

People are the purpose. Do not reduce users, customers, volunteers, churches, families, teams, or communities into engagement metrics or extraction targets.

## App Boundaries

Shared philosophy may guide every app, but each app needs its own charter, purpose, boundaries, audience, and success definition. Do not let one app's goals bleed into another app unless the connection is intentional and documented.

Apps share philosophy. Apps do not share purpose.

## Small Useful Action

Prefer the smallest useful action that moves a person, team, business, church, or community forward. Good systems turn insight into action, action into strength, and strength into multiplication.

## Safety

Do not expose secrets. Do not deploy directly from agent workflows. Prefer pull requests, preview deployments, reviewable changes, and explicit approval.
