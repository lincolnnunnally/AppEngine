# App Purpose Rules

## Core Rule

Apps share philosophy.

Apps do not share purpose.

Shared philosophy does not mean shared purpose. The Life Produces Life ecosystem can guide every app while each app keeps its own charter, audience, barriers, workflow, success definition, and boundaries.

## Purpose Examples

- Spark of Hope: hope.
- Live On Mission: action.
- Kindred Connection: belonging.
- Best Life: flourishing.
- ChurchConnect: coordination.
- Child First Solutions: family stability.
- Kids Need Dads: father restoration.
- Toner Management: stewardship and efficiency.
- EZPZ Site: simplicity and communication.
- Snip.Show: content multiplication.

## Direct Transformation Tools

Direct Transformation Tools directly help people move through transformation.

Examples:

- Spark of Hope.
- Live On Mission.
- Best Life.
- Kindred Connection.
- Child First Solutions.
- Kids Need Dads.

## Support Tools

Support Tools help organizations, churches, businesses, ministries, or people create environments where transformation can occur.

Examples:

- Toner Management.
- EZPZ Site.
- Snip.Show.
- Laser Engraving.
- Church infrastructure tools.

Both Direct Transformation Tools and Support Tools are valid.

Both belong in the ecosystem.

Agents must not force every app into a ministry-style application.

## Anti-Bleed Rule

Agents must prevent purpose bleed between apps.

Do not import another app's audience, workflows, data, brand promise, admin model, growth loop, or emotional posture unless a source-of-truth file explicitly approves the connection.

## Required Agent Behavior

- Load the app charter before planning, designing, building, reviewing, releasing, or improving an app.
- Name the active app purpose in the Context Gate.
- Classify the app as a Direct Transformation Tool, Support Tool, or explicitly mixed tool before implementation.
- Name what the app must not become.
- Treat cross-app ideas as integrations or follow-up issues, not silent scope expansion.
- Route existing-app improvements through vNext packets so they do not restart the app or absorb another app's purpose.
- Challenge requests where the purpose is unclear, ambiguous, or mixed across multiple apps.
