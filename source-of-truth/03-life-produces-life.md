# Life Produces Life Product Doctrine

Life Produces Life is not only a ministry slogan. It is a design, business, product, and systems principle.

It guides how AppEngine designs apps, businesses, workflows, community systems, growth loops, and improvement paths.

The apps are not the product. Transformation is the product.

People are not the product. People are the purpose.

## Core Pattern

Spark -> Serve -> Strength -> Best Life -> Multiply Life

## Human Journey

Survival -> Hope -> Action -> Discovery -> Becoming -> Thriving -> Multiplication

Purpose is often revealed through participation.

People do not need all the answers before they begin.

Hope -> Action -> Discovery

Not:

Hope -> Understanding -> Action

Agents should therefore prefer small useful actions that help people move from survival toward life, instead of waiting for perfect certainty or building oversized systems.

## Community Cycle

Struggle -> Spark of Hope -> Belonging -> Transformation -> Testimony -> Mission -> Growth -> Create -> Invite

The Human Journey and Community Cycle are complementary models, not competing models.

The Human Journey describes how a person moves. The Community Cycle describes how life can multiply through relationships, testimony, mission, growth, creation, and invitation.

## Important Correction

Do not treat service or sharing as only the final step after someone has hope or healing.

Service can be the beginning of healing. A person may find purpose, hope, identity, and strength by becoming useful and helping someone else.

## Applies To

- Ministry apps
- Business apps
- Community apps
- Internal workflow tools
- Customer service systems
- Growth strategy
- Generated app build packets
- Existing app vNext improvements

## Product Questions

Every app should answer:

1. Who is stuck, underserved, overwhelmed, or blocked?
2. What spark starts movement?
3. What barrier does this remove?
4. What need does this address?
5. What small act of service or action can move them forward now?
6. How does that action strengthen the user, team, business, church, or community?
7. What does Best Life look like in this app's specific context?
8. How does this help someone become a source of life for others?
9. How can the solution multiply life without forcing the app to become something outside its charter?
10. What must this app not become?
